FIX-006 · TOWER DEFENSE

FIX — Fixed Shooter Prototype #006 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Defend the kingdom by building towers on fixed spots along the path. Enemies march from the top-left toward your base at the bottom-right. Lose lives when enemies reach the end.

  • Click empty build spot — open tower menu
  • 1-4 — quick-select tower type
  • Click existing tower — upgrade menu
  • Click on path — move hero
  • H or click hero — hero special ability (area attack)
  • Space — send next wave
  • R — restart
  • ? — toggle this info panel

Towers

  • Archer (70g) — Fast attack, long range, low damage
  • Barracks (70g) — Spawns 3 soldiers that block enemies on path
  • Mage (100g) — Magic damage, effective vs armored
  • Bombard (125g) — Slow, area damage, devastating vs groups

Each tower can be upgraded 3 times for increased stats.

Enemies

  • Soldier — Basic infantry
  • Armored — Resistant to physical damage
  • Dark Mage — Fast, takes magic damage normally
  • Troll — Tanky boss unit
  • Wolf Rider — Very fast

History

Kingdom Rush (2011) by Ironhide Game Studio perfected the tower defense formula by adding a controllable hero unit and melee barracks that physically block enemies on the path. The game drew from WarCraft III custom maps that invented the genre, adding polish and the critical innovation of giving the player one direct-control unit amid automated defenses.

AI Archaeology

ERA 5 — Deep Learning. Tower defense is a multi-objective optimization problem: maximize damage output while managing gold economy and spatial coverage. The hero unit introduces a human-in-the-loop element — most of the defense is automated, but the player's micro-decisions with the hero can turn critical moments. This mirrors modern AI systems where automated processes handle routine work while humans intervene at decision points.

Prototype Notes

Barracks soldiers blocking enemies is the core tactical innovation — it transforms tower defense from pure placement optimization into a game about creating chokepoints and buying time. The hero adds micro-level decision-making on top of macro tower strategy.
