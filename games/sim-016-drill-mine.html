SIM-016 · DRILL MINE

SIM — Simulation Prototype #016 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Pilot your mining pod on Mars. Drill downward through layers of dirt and rock to collect valuable minerals, then return to the surface to sell them and upgrade your equipment.

  • ← → — Move left/right (drills through dirt)
  • ↓ — Drill downward
  • ↑ — Fly upward (uses extra fuel)
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

The Loop

  • Dig down to find minerals — deeper = rarer and more valuable
  • Watch your fuel gauge — running out means you are stranded!
  • Return to the surface to sell minerals at the shop
  • Buy upgrades: better drill, bigger fuel tank, stronger hull, more cargo space
  • Dig deeper with your new equipment

Minerals

  • Iron (grey) — Common, $5 each. Found near the surface.
  • Gold (yellow) — Medium rarity, $25 each. Below depth 20.
  • Diamond (cyan) — Rare, $100 each. Below depth 45.
  • Emerald (green) — Very rare, $250 each. Below depth 70.

Hazards

  • Lava — Damages your hull on contact. Appears at greater depths.
  • Falling — Drilling removes support; falling too far damages hull.

History

Motherload was created by XGen Studios in 2004 as a Flash browser game. Players controlled a mining machine on Mars, drilling through layers of earth to collect minerals and upgrade their equipment. The game became one of the most popular Flash games of its era, spawning a commercial sequel, Super Motherload (2013), which added cooperative multiplayer and procedurally generated worlds.

The downward-mining loop influenced numerous indie games including SteamWorld Dig (2013), Dome Keeper (2022), and countless mining-themed idle games. The core tension — how deep do you dare go before turning back — remains one of gaming's most compelling risk/reward loops.

AI Archaeology

The mining loop is a near-perfect illustration of exploration-exploitation tradeoffs studied in reinforcement learning. Each descent is a sequential decision problem: the agent must balance the diminishing marginal returns of surface minerals against the uncertain but potentially high rewards at depth, all while managing a depleting resource (fuel). This maps directly to the multi-armed bandit problem with resource constraints.

Prototype Notes

This prototype captures the essential Motherload loop: dig, collect, surface, sell, upgrade, repeat. The map is procedurally generated with mineral density increasing with depth. The upgrade system provides satisfying progression while fuel management maintains tension throughout each run.
