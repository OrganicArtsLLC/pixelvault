PUZ-007 · WORD GRID

PUZ — Puzzle Prototype #007 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Place tiles on the board to form valid crossword-style words. Score points based on letter values and premium squares. Play against an AI opponent.

  • Click a tile in your rack, then click a board cell to place it
  • Enter — Submit your word
  • Backspace — Recall all placed tiles back to rack
  • Tab — Exchange tiles (skips your turn)
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Scoring

  • DL (blue) — Double Letter Score
  • TL (cyan) — Triple Letter Score
  • DW (pink) — Double Word Score
  • TW (red) — Triple Word Score
  • ★ (center) — Double Word Score on first turn
  • Premium squares only apply when first covered

Tips

  • First word must cross the center star
  • Subsequent words must connect to existing tiles
  • All formed words (including cross-words) must be valid
  • High-value letters on premium squares maximize score

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Scrabble (1948, Alfred Mosher Butts) → Words with Friends (2009, Zynga) → Literati (1996, Yahoo)

Alfred Mosher Butts invented Scrabble during the Great Depression by combining crossword puzzles with anagram games. He studied letter frequency in newspapers to determine tile distribution and point values. The game was initially called "Lexiko" then "Criss-Crosswords" before becoming Scrabble in 1948. It became one of the best-selling board games in history.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion

Scrabble AI is a constraint satisfaction problem disguised as a word game. The AI must simultaneously satisfy lexical validity, board connectivity, and tile availability. Early Scrabble programs like Maven (1986) used DAWG (Directed Acyclic Word Graph) data structures for efficient word lookup. Modern Scrabble AI can consistently beat world champions.

Classification

Mechanic: Crossword-style word placement on premium-square grid against AI

Genre: Word Puzzle / Strategy

Prototype Notes

Compact word list of ~1500 common English words. AI uses constraint-based search to find valid placements. Premium square layout matches standard Scrabble board. Simplified rules — no challenges, no blank tiles.
