SCR-002 · CAVE SCROLL

SCR — Scrolling Shooter Prototype #002 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Fly over hostile terrain, destroy ground targets and enemies. The world scrolls relentlessly. Bomb fuel depots to refuel.

  • ← → ↑ ↓ or WASD — Move ship
  • Space — Fire forward laser
  • Z — Drop bomb (affected by gravity)
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Fuel: Depletes constantly. Bomb fuel depots (red tanks) to refuel.
  • Bombs drop: Bombs arc downward with gravity — lead your targets.
  • Terrain kills: Touching ground or ceiling is instant death.
  • Rockets: Ground rockets launch upward when you're above them.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Scramble (1981, Konami) → Super Cobra (1981) → Gradius (1985)

Scramble (1981) by Konami was the first forced-scrolling horizontal shooter. Unlike Defender (where the player controls scroll direction), Scramble forces the environment to move — the game proceeds whether you're ready or not. This created an entirely new type of challenge: planning ahead through a scrolling viewport.

The dual weapon system (forward laser + downward bomb) was elegant in its simplicity: two actions, but their utility depends entirely on the spatial relationship between player and target.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1978–1983)

Contemporary AI: Planning under uncertainty was a nascent field. Real-time systems were limited to reactive control.

Hidden Intelligence: Forced scrolling creates a real-time planning problem with a partially observable future. The scrolling horizon reveals obstacles just in time — the player must plan trajectories through terrain gaps while managing fuel and threats. This is an online planning problem under resource constraints, a class of problem that modern MCTS and model-predictive control address.

The Convergence: Modern autonomous vehicle navigation through constrained environments (narrow roads, tunnels) faces the same planning challenge: limited lookahead, obstacle avoidance, resource management, all in real-time.

If AI Had Designed This: Procedurally generated terrain with difficulty curves tuned by simulation — ensuring each section is survivable but demands optimal trajectories.

Classification

Series: SCR — Scrolling Shooter

Games where the environment moves continuously. Scramble adds terrain collision and fuel management to the scrolling formula.

Core Mechanic

Forced-scroll with dual weapons and fuel. Horizontal auto-scroll. Forward laser for air targets. Gravity bombs for ground targets. Fuel depletion as survival pressure.

What This Prototype Tests
