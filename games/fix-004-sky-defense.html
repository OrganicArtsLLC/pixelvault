FIX-004 · SKY DEFENSE

FIX — Fixed Shooter Prototype #004 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Defend your 6 cities from incoming ICBMs. Click to launch counter-missiles that explode at the cursor position. Intercept enemy missiles before they reach the ground.

  • Left click — Fire from left base
  • Right click — Fire from right base
  • Middle click or Space — Fire from center base
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Lead your targets: Counter-missiles take time to reach the cursor.
  • Explosions linger: The blast radius persists — use it to catch multiple missiles.
  • Ammo is limited: Each base has 10 missiles per wave. Use them wisely.
  • MIRVs: Later waves have missiles that split into multiple warheads.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Missile Command (1980, Dave Theurer, Atari) → Missile Defense 3-D (1983)

Missile Command was designed by Dave Theurer at Atari during the height of Cold War nuclear anxiety. Theurer reportedly had nightmares about nuclear annihilation while developing it. The game's emotional core — watching cities you can't save get destroyed — makes it one of the few early arcade games with genuine pathos.

The trackball cursor control and explosion-radius mechanic were innovative: instead of hitting targets directly, you create blast zones that intercept. This area-denial design is still used in modern tower defense games.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1978–1983)

Contemporary AI: Operations research was mature. Resource allocation under uncertainty was a well-studied problem. But real-time multi-target interception was beyond computational capability.

Hidden Intelligence: Missile Command is a resource allocation problem under escalating load. The player must decide which threats to intercept and which cities to sacrifice. This is the defender's dilemma: limited defense vs unlimited offense. Game theory's asymmetric warfare concept, instantiated as an arcade game. The escalating waves create a curriculum of increasing difficulty — the game teaches you before it kills you.

The Convergence: Modern missile defense systems (Patriot, Iron Dome) solve essentially this same problem computationally: track multiple incoming threats, allocate limited interceptors optimally, accept some targets will get through. The arcade game is a simplified simulation of the real problem.

If AI Had Designed This: Optimal interception planning using the same algorithms that real missile defense systems employ — minimizing expected casualties given interceptor counts and threat trajectories.

Classification

Series: FIX — Fixed Shooter

Missile Command extends the fixed-position paradigm from shooting at formation to defending an area. The player doesn't move — the cursor is the weapon.

Core Mechanic

Area defense with cursor-guided explosions. Three missile bases with limited ammo. Explosion radius as interception zone. Escalating ICBM waves. City survival as objective.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Cursor-guided explosions — does the area-denial feel satisfying?
  • Resource anxiety — does limited ammo create meaningful decisions?
  • City attachment — does losing a city feel like a loss?
  • Escalation — do later waves feel overwhelming in a fair way?
