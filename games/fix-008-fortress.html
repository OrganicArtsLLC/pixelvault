FIX-008 · FORTRESS (포트리스)

FIX — Fixed Shooter Prototype #008 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Team artillery battle: you and an AI ally vs two AI enemies. Tanks take turns firing across hilly terrain with wind affecting every shot. Destroy all enemy tanks to win.

  • Up / Down — adjust cannon angle
  • Space (hold & release) — charge and fire (longer hold = more power)
  • 1 — Standard shot (default)
  • 2 — Double shot (two projectiles)
  • 3 — Spread shot (three-way fan)
  • 4 — Laser (straight beam, ignores wind)
  • R — restart game
  • ? — toggle this info panel

Wind

The wind arrow at the top of the screen shows direction and strength. Wind changes every turn. Standard, double, and spread shots are all affected — only the laser ignores wind. Reading the wind is the core skill.

Weapons

  • Standard (1) — Reliable single shot, unlimited ammo
  • Double (2) — Two projectiles in quick succession, 3 uses
  • Spread (3) — Three projectiles in a fan pattern, 2 uses
  • Laser (4) — Instant straight-line beam ignoring wind, 1 use

History

Fortress (포트리스) was released by CCR Inc. in 1999 and became a cultural phenomenon in South Korea's PC bang (internet cafe) scene. Its accessible turn-based artillery gameplay with wind mechanics made it perfect for casual social gaming. At its peak, millions of Korean players gathered in PC bangs to play Fortress, making it one of the earliest massively popular online games in Korea — predating the StarCraft esports boom. The game ran for over a decade, spawning sequels (Fortress 2, Fortress 2 Blue) and cementing team artillery as a beloved Korean genre alongside Crazy Arcade and KartRider.

AI Archaeology

Wind-compensated artillery AI must solve inverse kinematics under uncertainty: given target position, gravity, and a randomly changing wind vector, compute the launch angle and power. The team element adds strategic depth — the AI must choose between finishing a wounded enemy or softening a healthy one, and decide when to spend limited special weapons versus conserving them.

Classification

Artillery / Turn-Based Tactics — team-based projectile combat with environmental physics (wind, terrain destruction). Part of the lineage from Artillery (1976) through Scorched Earth to Worms, with Fortress adding the wind-every-turn mechanic and Korean social gaming culture.

Prototype Notes

This prototype captures the core 2v2 team artillery loop with destructible terrain, wind variation per turn, and four weapon types. The original Fortress featured dozens of tank types with unique special attacks; this sketch distills the essential wind-reading skill that made every shot in Korean PC bangs a communal spectacle.
