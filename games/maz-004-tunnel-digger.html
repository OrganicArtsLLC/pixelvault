MAZ-004 · TUNNEL DIGGER

MAZ — Maze + Chase Prototype #004 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Dig tunnels through dirt and eliminate enemies by inflating them with your pump until they pop. Drop rocks on enemies for bonus points.

  • ↑ ↓ ← → — Move / Dig
  • Space — Shoot pump harpoon / Hold to inflate enemy
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Pump timing: Press Space to shoot your harpoon. If it connects, hold Space to inflate the enemy. Release and they slowly deflate.
  • Rock drops: Dig beneath a rock to make it fall. It crushes any enemy below it for bonus points.
  • Ghost mode: Enemies occasionally turn into ghosts and pass through dirt to chase you. Stay alert!
  • Depth bonus: Enemies deeper in the field are worth more points. The dirt color changes by layer.
  • Last enemy: The final enemy will try to flee to the surface. Chase it down!

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Dig Dug (1982, Namco) → Mr. Do! (1982) → Boulder Dash (1984)

Dig Dug was designed by Shouichi Fukatani and released by Namco in 1982. It was revolutionary for letting the player reshape the playing field — tunneling through dirt created an ever-changing maze. The game sold over 22,000 arcade units in its first year.

The pump mechanic was uniquely tactile for its era: players physically held the button to inflate enemies, creating a visceral connection between input and on-screen action. The dual elimination methods (pumping and rock-dropping) gave players strategic choice.

Fygar, the fire-breathing dragon, was one of the earliest enemies with a directional ranged attack, forcing players to consider approach angles — a spatial reasoning challenge ahead of its time.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1980-1987)

Contemporary AI: Expert systems maturing, early spatial reasoning research.

Hidden Intelligence: Dig Dug's enemies exhibit two behavioral modes — normal tunnel-following pathfinding and ghost-mode wall penetration. This is a state-machine AI with environment-dependent transitions. The enemy decides when to "phase out" based on distance to the player and time spent chasing through tunnels — an early form of context-aware decision-making.

The Convergence: The ghost-mode mechanic anticipates modern AI agent architectures where agents switch between behavioral modes based on environmental context. The enemy's ability to "break the rules" (pass through walls) when frustrated mirrors exploration strategies in reinforcement learning.

If AI Had Designed This: Adaptive ghost-mode triggers calibrated to player skill — more frequent phasing for skilled players who efficiently dig escape routes, creating a difficulty curve that emerges from the AI's behavioral switching.

Classification

Series: MAZ — Maze + Chase

Dig Dug inverts the traditional maze game: instead of navigating an existing maze, the player creates the maze. The tunnels you dig become the pathways enemies follow — and your escape routes.

Core Mechanic

Tunnel through dirt, pump enemies to pop them. Player-created topology. Dual elimination methods (pump + rock). Enemy state-machine AI with ghost mode. Depth-based scoring. Physics-based rock drops.
