SHT-008 · CELL ABSORB

SHT — Shooter Prototype #008 ★★★★☆

How to Play

You are a cell in a petri dish arena. Eat food pellets and smaller cells to grow. Avoid anything bigger than you — it will eat you.

  • Mouse — Move toward cursor
  • WASD — Move (alternative)
  • Space — Split: launch half your mass forward to eat distant prey
  • W — Eject a small mass pellet (bait or feed allies)
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Size is power: You can only eat cells smaller than you. Bigger cells move slower.
  • Splitting: High risk, high reward — you become two smaller (vulnerable) cells but the launched half can snag prey.
  • Mass decay: Larger cells lose mass faster over time, creating natural balance.
  • Ejecting: Press W to eject a small pellet — useful as bait to lure AI cells.
  • Merging: After splitting, your cells will merge back together after a cooldown.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Agar.io (2015, Matheus Valadares)

Agar.io was created by 19-year-old Brazilian developer Matheus Valadares and posted to 4chan in April 2015. Within weeks it became a global phenomenon, peaking at over 100,000 concurrent players. Its genius lay in radical simplicity: a browser game with no login, no tutorial, and one rule — bigger eats smaller.

The game spawned the entire .io genre: Slither.io, Diep.io, Surviv.io, and hundreds more. It proved that browser-based multiplayer games with minimal graphics could captivate millions, and that emergent gameplay from simple rules could rival designed complexity.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 7 — Self-Play & Emergent

Agar.io is a natural laboratory for emergent behavior. With no designed strategies, players independently discovered teaming, split-kills, virus feeding, corner trapping, and bait tactics. The game's ecosystem mirrors predator-prey dynamics studied in artificial life research.

AI agents trained on Agar.io-like environments develop sophisticated strategies through self-play: they learn to hunt in packs, set traps, and sacrifice mass strategically — all from the single reward signal of "grow bigger." This parallels how simple fitness functions in evolutionary AI produce unexpectedly complex behaviors.

Prototype Notes

Reconstructs the core Agar.io loop: eat food, grow, eat smaller cells, avoid bigger ones, split to attack. 15-20 AI cells provide the living ecosystem. The split mechanic creates the game's signature risk/reward moment — halving yourself to snag a kill. Mass decay prevents indefinite growth and keeps the arena dynamic.
