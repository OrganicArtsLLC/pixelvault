PUZ — Puzzle Prototype #027 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Roll out Persian carpets to build paths across treacherous rooms. Guide your character to the exit door.

  • Click a carpet (colored rectangle on the grid) to select it
  • Click a direction arrow that appears to unroll the carpet that way
  • W/A/S/D or Arrow keys — Move your character on walkable tiles and carpets
  • R — Restart current level
  • N — Next level (after completing current one)
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Rules

  • Carpets unroll in one direction to their fixed length (2, 3, or 4 tiles)
  • Carpets bridge water gaps and cover spike hazards
  • Carpets cannot overlap other carpets or walls
  • Once unrolled, a carpet stays in place
  • Reach the golden exit door to complete each level

History

Farsh was created by Iranian game designer Mahdi Bahrami in Tehran in 2017. The word "farsh" (فرش) means carpet in Persian. The game transforms the familiar act of rolling out a carpet into an elegant puzzle mechanic, drawing on Iran's deep cultural connection to carpet weaving — an art form dating back over 2,500 years.

Bahrami's design philosophy emphasizes finding game mechanics in everyday actions. The fixed-length carpet constraint creates surprisingly deep puzzles from a simple verb: unroll.

Insight

Each carpet is a rigid body with fixed length — the player must mentally simulate unrolling sequences to construct viable paths. This mirrors constraint-propagation problems in AI, where each placement decision constrains future possibilities.

Prototype Notes

8 hand-crafted levels with progressive difficulty. Persian carpet aesthetic with geometric border patterns rendered procedurally on canvas. The rolling animation unfurls carpets tile-by-tile for tactile satisfaction.

Educational Notice

This is an educational prototype created for study and preservation purposes. It demonstrates the carpet-rolling path puzzle mechanic pioneered by Mahdi Bahrami. This is not the original game — it is a minimal, non-commercial reconstruction for understanding game design history.
