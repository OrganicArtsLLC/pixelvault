PLT — Platformer Prototype #022 ★★★★☆

How to Play

You are a small green slime ball. You cannot walk — you can only launch, stick to surfaces, and possess enemies. Body-hopping IS your traversal.

  • Mouse — Aim launch direction (or use arrow keys)
  • Click / Space — Launch slime toward cursor
  • Land on an enemy to possess them
  • ← → — Move possessed host
  • ↑ — Jump (soldier) / Climb (spider) / Fly up (flyer)
  • Space — Shoot (soldier only)
  • Z — Detach from host (return to slime)
  • R — Restart current room
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Host Types

  • Soldier: Walks on ground, can jump. Press Space to shoot. Needed to destroy barriers.
  • Spider: Climbs walls and ceilings, moves fast. Can reach areas others cannot.
  • Flyer: Hovers and flies freely. Can reach high platforms and cross gaps.

Tips

  • The slime sticks to any solid surface — walls and ceilings too.
  • You must detach (Z) before you can possess a new host.
  • Each room requires specific host abilities to reach the exit door.
  • Look for the green exit door to advance to the next room.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: MO:Astray (2019, Archpray) ← Gish (2004, Chronic Logic) ← experimental slime platformers

MO:Astray (2019) was developed by Archpray, a team spanning Korea and Taiwan. In it, players control a sentient slime creature navigating a sci-fi facility, possessing host bodies to solve puzzles and traverse dangerous environments. The game blends horror atmosphere with inventive platforming mechanics.

The body-possession mechanic creates a unique form of platforming where the player character itself has limited mobility, but gains radically different movement and combat abilities through the bodies it inhabits. Each possession transforms the gameplay, requiring players to think about which host suits each situation.

Its lineage traces through Gish (2004), Edmund McMillen’s physics-based slime platformer, which explored non-humanoid protagonists with elastic, sticky movement. MO:Astray advanced the concept by making body-switching the core puzzle mechanic rather than just a movement novelty.

Prototype Notes

This prototype implements the core body-possession loop across 4 puzzle rooms. The slime launches, sticks, and possesses three host types (soldier, spider, flyer), each with distinct abilities needed to reach the exit. Room design requires strategic host-switching to progress.
