How to Play

Survive 8 rapid-fire minigames! Each lasts 10–20 seconds with unique controls:

  • BUTTON MASH — Pound Space to sprint to the finish
  • TIMING — Press Space when the bar is in the green zone
  • MEMORY — Watch the color sequence, repeat with 1–4
  • DODGE — Use ←→ to dodge falling objects
  • COUNTING — Count the items and type the number
  • RHYTHM — Press Space on each beat marker
  • GROWTH — Mash Space to inflate — but stop before it pops!
  • STACK — Press Space to drop blocks and stack them aligned

Score accumulates across all minigames. The wackier you play, the more fun you have!

History

Bishi Bashi Special was released by Konami in 1996 as an arcade game in Japan. Its formula of absurd, lightning-fast minigames — from pie-eating contests to hairdressing races — became a beloved party game format. The series directly inspired Nintendo’s WarioWare (2003) and established the “microgame compilation” as a legitimate genre. The original arcade cabinets featured large colorful buttons designed for frantic mashing.

Prototype Notes

This prototype captures the core Bishi Bashi loop: rapid context switches between trivially simple mechanics that become challenging through pace and variety. Eight minigames cover the major interaction types — mashing, timing, memory, spatial awareness, counting, rhythm, risk management, and precision. The announcer text between games recreates the joyful absurdity of the original.
