How to Play

Korean traditional wrestling. Two wrestlers grip each other's satba (샅바) waist band. Force your opponent off-balance and make any part of their body above the knee touch the sand to win. Best of 3 rounds.

History & Lineage

Ssireum (씨름) is Korea's oldest traditional sport, with origins stretching back at least to the 4th century CE. The earliest known depictions appear in the tomb murals of the Goguryeo kingdom (37 BCE – 668 CE) at Anak and Jiangchuan, showing two wrestlers locked in the characteristic grip.

The sport is defined by the satba (샅바), a cloth band worn around the waist and right thigh. Both wrestlers must grip the opponent's satba before the match begins. Victory comes when any part of the opponent's body above the knee touches the ground — matches are explosive, often lasting under 15 seconds.

Historically performed at Dano (단오) harvest festivals, ssireum was a community celebration with the champion winning a bull as prize. Professional ssireum leagues flourished in South Korea from the 1980s-2000s. In 2018, ssireum was inscribed as UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage through a historic joint nomination by North and South Korea — the first such joint inscription.

Prototype Notes

This prototype captures the core push-pull-trip-lift loop of ssireum. The satba grip is abstracted into the permanent connection between wrestlers. The sandy ring (모래판, moraepan) is rendered as a circular arena. Sound effects use Web Audio API synthesis for grunts, sand shuffling, and crowd reactions.

Educational Notice: This prototype is a simplified simulation for educational purposes. Ssireum is a rich martial art with hundreds of named techniques across multiple categories (son-gisul hand techniques, bal-gisul leg techniques, heori-gisul waist techniques). This prototype abstracts these into four core actions. For authentic ssireum, see the Korea Ssireum Association and UNESCO's intangible heritage documentation.
