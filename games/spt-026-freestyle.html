SPT — Sports Prototype #026 ★★★★☆

How to Play

1v1 half-court street basketball. First to 11 points wins.

  • ← → ↑ ↓ — Move player
  • Space — Shoot (hold to charge power, release at jump peak for best accuracy)
  • Z — Pass/Steal (steal on defense)
  • X — Special dribble move (crossover/spin to fake out defender)
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

On offense: dribble toward the basket, use X for crossover/spin moves to shake the defender, then shoot with good timing. On defense: stay between opponent and basket, press Z to steal, Space to block shots.

Shots inside the arc score 2 points, outside the arc score 3 points. Timing and distance from the basket affect shot accuracy. The defender's proximity also impacts your shooting percentage.

History

Freestyle (프리스타일, 2005) was developed by JCE Entertainment in South Korea. It became one of the most popular online games in Asia, blending arcade basketball with MMO-style character progression. Its timing-based shooting system — requiring players to release at the exact peak of their jump — created a high skill ceiling that rewarded practice.

The game drew from the NBA Street series and Korean street basketball culture, emphasizing flashy moves and individual skill over simulation realism. Its free-to-play model helped popularize the business model in Korea.

AI Archaeology

Defensive AI

The AI defender tracks your offensive tendencies. If you consistently drive to one side, the AI will shade its positioning to cut off that lane. Varying your approach — mixing drives, pull-up jumpers, and crossovers — is essential to scoring consistently.

Shot Timing System

The shooting mechanic uses a physics-based arc where releasing at the jump apex provides maximum accuracy. This mirrors Freestyle's signature "green release" timing window that separated elite players from beginners.

Prototype Notes

This prototype captures the core 1v1 half-court experience with timing-based shooting and dribble moves. The full game featured 3v3 matches, character classes (Guard, Forward, Center), and extensive cosmetic customization.
