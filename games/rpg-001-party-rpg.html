RPG-001 · PARTY RPG

RPG — Role-Playing Prototype #001 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Lead a party of four adventurers through a 3-level dungeon. Navigate corridors in first-person, fight monsters in turn-based combat, and defeat the boss on level 3 to win.

  • ↑ — Move forward
  • ← / → — Turn left / right
  • ↓ — Step backward
  • 1-4 — Select party member
  • Space — Attack / confirm selection
  • D — Defend (in combat)
  • S — Cast spell (in combat, if MP available)
  • F — Flee from combat
  • R — Restart (on game over/victory)
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Combat

  • Select a party member with 1-4, then choose action.
  • Warrior: High HP and attack. No spells.
  • Rogue: Medium stats. 25% critical hit chance for double damage.
  • Mage: Low HP, high magic. Fireball hits all enemies.
  • Healer: Medium stats. Heal spell restores party HP.
  • Find treasure chests for gold and XP. Level up to increase stats.
  • Find stairs down to descend deeper. Defeat the boss on level 3.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Wizardry (1981, Sir-Tech) → The Bard's Tale (1985, Interplay) → Dungeon Master (1987) → Eye of the Beholder (1991)

The Bard's Tale (1985) was a landmark CRPG that brought the first-person dungeon crawl to a wider audience. Designed by Michael Cranford and published by Interplay, it built on the wireframe corridors of Wizardry but added color, a persistent overworld city, and party-based tactical combat.

The first-person dungeon crawl format creates a unique tension: the player sees only what's directly ahead. Every turn around a corner could reveal treasure or death. This limited visibility transforms spatial navigation into an information-gathering problem — the player must build a mental (or paper) map through sequential observation.

Party management adds a layer of multi-agent coordination. Each character has a role — tank, damage, healing, utility — and the player must orchestrate their actions as a team, managing shared resources like hit points and spell slots.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1980-1993)

Contemporary AI: Expert systems, early knowledge representation. The era when AI researchers believed capturing domain expertise in rule systems could solve complex problems.

Hidden Intelligence: The dungeon crawl is a partially observable Markov decision process (POMDP). The player has incomplete state knowledge (unexplored map) and must choose actions (move, turn, fight) that simultaneously gather information and pursue goals. Each step is a tradeoff between exploration and exploitation.

Party combat is a multi-agent coordination problem. Four characters with complementary abilities must act in sequence, and the optimal action for each depends on the others' choices. This is the same structure as multi-robot task allocation in modern AI.

The Convergence: Modern game AI uses Monte Carlo tree search for dungeon generation and reinforcement learning for combat balancing — the same problems players solve intuitively.

If AI Had Designed This: Procedurally calibrated encounters tuned by RL agents to maintain a target difficulty curve — keeping tension without frustration. The dungeon layout would maximize information uncertainty per tile to keep exploration rewarding.

Core Mechanic

First-person dungeon crawl with party-based turn combat. Grid movement in 3D perspective. Random encounters. Four-member party with class roles. Level progression. Multi-floor dungeon with boss.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Combat pacing — does turn-based combat flow without tedium?

  • Progression arc — does leveling up feel meaningful across 3 dungeon levels?
