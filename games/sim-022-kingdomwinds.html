SIM-022 · KINGDOM OF THE WINDS

SIM — Simulation Prototype #022 ★★★★☆

How to Play

You are a warrior of the ancient Goguryeo kingdom. Explore the village, accept quests from NPCs, venture into the wilderness, slay monsters, and grow stronger.

  • Arrow Keys — Move your warrior
  • Space — Attack nearby monsters
  • E — Talk to NPCs / interact
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Quest Loop

  • Talk to NPCs in the village to receive quests
  • Venture into the forest and dungeon to fight monsters
  • Defeat monsters to gain EXP and collect loot
  • Return to the quest NPC to claim your reward
  • Level up to increase your stats and take on harder areas

History

The Kingdom of the Winds (바람의나라, Baram-ui Nara, 1996) is Korea's first graphical online game, created by Jake Song at Nexon. Based on Kim Jin's manhwa of the same name, set in the ancient Goguryeo kingdom of Korean mythology.

The game established the template for Korean MMOs: top-down tile graphics, real-time combat, quest-driven progression, and persistent online worlds. Jake Song went on to create Lineage (1998) at NCsoft, which became the most commercially successful online game in Asia and the direct ancestor of virtually every Korean online RPG that followed.

Kingdom of the Winds proved that graphical multiplayer RPGs could work at scale in Asia, predating Ultima Online by a year. It remains operational today — one of the longest-running online games in history.

AI Archaeology

This prototype captures the fundamental MMO loop that Jake Song pioneered: village → quest → hunt → loot → return → level up. The quest-kill-loot-return cycle became the foundational grammar of online RPGs worldwide. The NPC dialogue trees, spawn systems, and stat progression shown here are the building blocks of a genre that would generate billions in revenue.

Classification

AI-ERA 4 — Statistical Learning. NPC behavior, monster spawning logic, and quest state machines represent early applications of finite state automata in game AI. The real-time combat system uses proximity detection and cooldown timers that would later evolve into sophisticated aggro and threat systems in modern MMOs.

Prototype Notes

Simplified single-player distillation. Three zones (village, forest, dungeon) connected by tile-based map transitions. Real-time combat with attack cooldowns. NPC quest system with kill-count tracking. Level-up stat increases. The Goguryeo aesthetic is represented through tile colors and character styling evocative of the manhwa's historical Korean setting.
