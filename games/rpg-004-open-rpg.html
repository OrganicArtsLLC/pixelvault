RPG — Role-Playing Prototype #004 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Explore a tile-based overworld, visit towns, delve into dungeons, and defeat the Dark Lord.

  • ↑ ↓ ← → — Move (tile-based, turn-based movement)
  • Space — Interact (talk to NPCs, open chests) / Attack (in combat)
  • I — Open/close inventory
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Overworld

  • Towns (T): Walk onto town tile. Face NPCs and press Space to talk. Press B to buy.
  • Dungeons (D): Walk onto dungeon tile. Fight monsters, find chests.
  • Monsters: Roaming enemies engage in combat when adjacent.
  • Terrain: Mountains and water are impassable.

Combat

  • Turn-based: When you bump a monster (or it bumps you), combat begins.
  • Space — Attack | D — Defend (half damage) | F — Flee (50% chance)
  • Defeat monsters for XP and gold. Level up at thresholds for stat boosts.

Tips

  • Visit the first town to talk to NPCs and learn about the world.
  • Buy potions before entering dungeons — healing is scarce.
  • Grind weaker monsters to level up before tackling dungeons.
  • The Dark Lord's fortress is in the northeast. You need to be strong to survive.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Ultima IV (1985, Origin Systems/Richard Garriott) ← Ultima I-III (1981-83) ← Akalabeth (1979)

Richard Garriott ("Lord British") created Ultima as a teenager. Ultima IV (1985) revolutionized RPGs by making moral virtue the win condition — the player had to embody eight virtues to become the Avatar. No final boss; consistent ethical behavior was the goal.

The series pioneered tile-based overworlds, NPC dialogue, and the open-world CRPG format that influenced Final Fantasy and The Elder Scrolls.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1980-1993)

Contemporary AI: Expert systems at peak hype. MYCIN, DENDRAL. Rule-based reasoning dominant.

Hidden Intelligence: Ultima IV's virtue system is a value-alignment problem — the game requires the player to consistently act according to ethical principles, not for reward, but because alignment IS the goal. This is the central challenge of modern AI alignment.

The NPC keyword dialogue is an early NLI — a lookup table masquerading as conversation, the same pattern underlying chatbots from ELIZA to ALICE.

The Convergence: The invisible virtue tracker anticipates RLHF reward modeling — shaping behavior through delayed, implicit feedback.

If AI Had Designed This: Procedural ethical dilemmas with dynamic NPC reactions based on reputation graphs — a social simulation driven by moral modeling.

Classification

Series: RPG — Role-Playing

Core Mechanic

Tile-based open world RPG. Overworld exploration, towns with NPCs and shops, dungeons with monsters and loot, turn-based combat. Stats: HP, MP, STR, DEX, INT, Level, XP, Gold.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Overworld exploration feel on a scrolling tile grid
  • Town NPC interaction and sense of a living world
  • Dungeon risk/reward decision-making
  • Turn-based combat tactical depth
  • Progression from weak adventurer to Dark Lord slayer

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. 30x30 tile overworld with varied terrain. Three towns with NPCs and shops. Two dungeons plus a final fortress. Turn-based combat system. Level progression with stat growth. Simple inventory with weapons, armor, and potions.
