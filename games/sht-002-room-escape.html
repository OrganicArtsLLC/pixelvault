SHT-002 · ROOM ESCAPE

SHT — Shooter Prototype #002 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Clear robots from each room. Exit through any doorway to enter a new room. Don't touch walls or robots. Flee Evil Otto — he cannot be killed.

  • WASD — Move
  • ↑ ↓ ← → — Shoot in that direction
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Walls kill: Touching any wall (even briefly) is lethal. Stay centered in corridors.
  • Evil Otto: Appears after a few seconds. He bounces and cannot be stopped. Leave the room.
  • Robot-on-robot: Robots can shoot each other and walk into walls. Use this to your advantage.
  • Doorways: Exit through any wall opening to generate a new room.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Berzerk (1980, Stern Electronics) → Frenzy (1982) → Smash TV (1990)

Berzerk (1980) was one of the first arcade games with speech synthesis ("Intruder alert!"). It introduced room-by-room arena combat — each screen is a self-contained puzzle with procedurally placed walls and robots.

Evil Otto — the bouncing smiley face that cannot be killed — is one of gaming's first unkillable pursuers. He creates an implicit timer: stay too long and death is certain. The combination of killable robots and unkillable Otto forces the player to triage threats, a multi-objective decision problem.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 2 — Expert Systems (1972–1980)

Contemporary AI: Rule-based systems. No concept of multi-objective decision-making in games.

Hidden Intelligence: The dual-threat design (killable robots + unkillable Otto) creates an asymmetric planning problem. The player must decide: fight or flee? Clear the room for points or exit safely? This is triage under time pressure — a multi-objective optimization problem where the objectives conflict. Modern AI formalizes this as Pareto-optimal decision-making.

The Convergence: Berzerk's room-based structure with procedural layouts anticipates roguelike design. Each room is a micro-episode, and the infinite room sequence resembles episodic RL training environments.

If AI Had Designed This: Procedurally optimized room layouts that maximize the tension between "clearable" and "must flee" — calibrating Evil Otto's arrival time to the expected room-clear time for a given layout.

Classification

Series: SHT — Shooter

Berzerk adds room transitions and an unkillable pursuer to the arena shooter formula. Movement and shooting are on separate input axes.

Core Mechanic

Room-by-room arena with dual-threat triage. Lethal walls. Robots that shoot back. Evil Otto as hard timer. Separate move and shoot controls. Procedural room generation.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Room transitions — does entering a new room feel like a fresh puzzle?
  • Dual-threat triage — does the fight-or-flee decision feel meaningful?
  • Wall lethality — does wall-touch-kills add tension to movement?
  • Evil Otto pressure — does his arrival create urgency without unfairness?

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. Procedural room layouts with internal walls. Seeded rooms from coordinates. Evil Otto spawns after delay.
