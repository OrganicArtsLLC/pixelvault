How to Play

Fast-paced 2D platform combat with grappling hook physics. Use the grapple to swing across the arena, build momentum, and outmaneuver your opponents. First to 10 kills wins the deathmatch!

  • A D — Move left / right
  • W — Jump
  • Mouse — Aim
  • Left Click — Shoot
  • Right Click or E — Fire / release grappling hook
  • 1 — Pistol (unlimited ammo)
  • 2 — Shotgun (spread, limited ammo)
  • 3 — Grenade launcher (bouncing grenades, limited ammo)
  • R — Restart match
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

History

Teeworlds (originally "Teewars") was created by Magnus Auvinen in 2007 as an open-source 2D multiplayer shooter. Its grappling hook mechanic, inherited from games like Liero and Soldat, became the defining feature — transforming simple platform movement into high-speed aerial acrobatics. The game built a dedicated competitive community around its physics-based skill ceiling.

AI Archaeology

Era 5 — Deep Learning: The grappling hook creates a continuous action space that defies simple rule-based AI. Optimal hook usage requires predicting momentum arcs, timing releases for maximum velocity, and chaining swings into fluid trajectories. This is precisely the kind of physics-based control problem where deep reinforcement learning excels — learning policies that map continuous state (position, velocity, hook angle) to continuous actions.

Classification

  • Genre: 2D Platform Fighter / Arena Shooter
  • Input: Keyboard + Mouse (aim + shoot + grapple)
  • Core loop: Grapple → build momentum → aim → shoot → respawn
  • Skill ceiling: Very high (grapple physics mastery)

Prototype Notes

This prototype focuses on making the grapple feel satisfying — momentum transfer, pendulum swings, and slingshot launches. AI opponents use grapple for basic traversal and attempt aerial combat. The arena is designed with vertical space to encourage grapple-based movement over ground walking.
