MAZ-016 · BURROW PUZZLE

MAZ — Maze + Chase Prototype #016 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Push the black ball to the exit hole on each screen. Use the two-layer mechanic to navigate around obstacles.

  • ↑ ↓ ← → — Move mole
  • Space — Dig down (on soft ground) / Dig up (underground at hole)
  • R — Restart current level
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

On the surface, walk around and push the ball. Stand on soft ground (brown patches) and press Space to dig underground.

Underground, move freely with no obstacles. Press Space on a hole marker to emerge back on the surface.

The trick: dig underground to bypass walls and rocks, emerge on the other side, and push the ball from a new angle.

History

Mole Mania was directed by Shigeru Miyamoto and released for the Game Boy in 1996. Despite Miyamoto's involvement, it received little marketing and modest sales. The game stars Muddy Mole, who must rescue his family from a farmer by solving screen-by-screen puzzles involving pushing, pulling, and digging.

The two-layer mechanic — surface and underground — was revolutionary for a puzzle game, creating spatial problems where the solution is often "go under it." Each screen is a self-contained puzzle requiring the player to think in two planes simultaneously.

AI Archaeology

Classification

ERA 3 — Knowledge Explosion. The two-layer puzzle design maps directly to multi-state search problems where an agent must reason about transitions between parallel state spaces.

AI Insight

Mole Mania's mechanic anticipates hierarchical planning: the mole must form a plan that spans two layers, choosing when to transition between them. Modern AI planners face analogous challenges in multi-modal reasoning — switching between different representations to solve problems that are intractable in a single space.

Prototype Notes

This prototype implements 8 puzzle screens with increasing complexity. The underground layer is rendered with a darker palette. Soft ground spots are marked with brown patches on the surface. The ball can only be pushed (not pulled) and cannot enter holes or pass through walls.
