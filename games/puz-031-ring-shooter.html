PUZ — Puzzle Prototype #031 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Shoot colored balls into a moving chain that winds along a spiral track. Match 3 or more same-colored balls to remove them. Clear the entire chain to advance. Don't let the chain reach the skull!

  • Mouse — Aim the launcher
  • Click — Shoot a ball
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Scoring

  • 3 balls matched: 100 points
  • Each additional ball: +50 bonus
  • Chain combos: 2x multiplier per combo
  • Level clear bonus: 500 × level

History

Puzz Loop was created by Mitchell Corporation in 1998 as a Japanese arcade game. It introduced the marble-chain mechanic where players shoot colored balls into a moving chain on a track, matching colors to eliminate segments. The game was a moderate arcade hit in Japan.

PopCap Games later popularized the mechanic with Zuma (2003), which became one of the most successful casual games ever made. The core mechanic — inserting into a chain and matching — proved to be one of the most satisfying puzzle interactions ever designed.

AI Archaeology

ERA 5 — Deep Learning Dawn. The chain-shooting mechanic creates an interesting AI problem: predicting where a projectile will intersect a moving chain on a curved path, then evaluating which insertion point creates the best match. Modern AI approaches this through trajectory simulation and lookahead search.

Prototype Notes

This prototype recreates the core Puzz Loop experience: a spiral track with advancing colored balls, a central launcher, and satisfying chain-pop mechanics. The track is procedurally generated as a spiral. Sound uses Web Audio API for pop, insert, and rumble effects.
