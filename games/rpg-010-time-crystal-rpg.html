RPG-010 · CRISTALES

RPG — Role-Playing Game Prototype #010 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Experience a time-split world: past, present, and future shown simultaneously across three panels. Explore in the present, then enter turn-based combat with time manipulation.

  • ←→ or AD — Move left/right
  • Space — Interact / Start combat encounter
  • 1 — Attack (in combat)
  • 2 — Poison Strike (in combat)
  • 3 — Shield (in combat)
  • Z — Send enemy to Past (weaken / accelerate effects)
  • X — Send enemy to Future (age / accelerate effects)
  • R — Restart
  • ? — This panel

History

Cris Tales (2021) by Dreams Uncorporated and SYCK, based in Bogotá, Colombia, became the country's first major internationally released game. Inspired by classic JRPGs like Chrono Trigger and Paper Mario, it introduced a unique time-split visual mechanic where players see past, present, and future simultaneously on screen. The game's art style drew from Colombian architecture, culture, and stained-glass cathedral aesthetics.

AI Archaeology

Era 7 — Foundation Models. The time-split screen is a spatial rendering of temporal causality — three parallel realities displayed simultaneously so the player perceives cause and effect as a visual relationship rather than a sequential one. Combat time manipulation creates a combinatorial puzzle space: applying status effects then shifting time creates emergent strategies (poison + future = accelerated damage, shield + past = retroactive protection). This transforms turn-based combat from a damage race into a temporal logic puzzle.

Prototype Notes

Captures the signature time-split display with three simultaneous panels showing past (sepia tones), present (full color), and future (blue tint). The exploration phase demonstrates environmental time differences. Combat features the core temporal manipulation mechanic: attack normally, apply status effects, then shift enemies through time to amplify or transform those effects. Four encounters culminating in a boss fight showcase the system's depth.
