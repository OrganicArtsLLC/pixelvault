PUZ — Puzzle Prototype #039 ★★★★☆

How to Play

A quiz-puzzle hybrid: answer trivia questions to attack your AI opponent with garbage blocks. Speed matters!

  • 1 2 3 4 — Select answer A, B, C, or D
  • Answer correctly and FAST to send more garbage blocks to the opponent
  • Answer wrong and garbage blocks fall on YOUR field instead
  • If blocks reach the top of either field, that player loses
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Scoring

  • Fast correct (under 3s) — 4 garbage rows sent to opponent
  • Medium correct (3-6s) — 2 garbage rows sent to opponent
  • Slow correct (6s+) — 1 garbage row sent to opponent
  • Wrong answer — 2 garbage rows fall on YOU
  • AI answers with variable speed and accuracy

History & Lineage

Ancestry: QuizQuiz (1999, Nexon) ← Puzzle Fighter (1996, Capcom) ← Quiz & Dragons (1992, Capcom)

QuizQuiz (퀴즈퀴즈) was released by Nexon in 1999 as one of the first — arguably THE first — free-to-play online games ever created. Players competed head-to-head answering trivia questions, with correct answers sending puzzle blocks raining down on opponents. Nexon, founded in South Korea in 1994, pioneered the F2P business model with QuizQuiz and later MapleStory, fundamentally reshaping how games are monetized worldwide. The "quiz as weapon" mechanic remains unique in gaming history.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 5 — Connected Intelligence

QuizQuiz sits at the intersection of knowledge retrieval and competitive gaming. The AI opponent must simulate human-like quiz performance — variable response times, knowledge gaps, and strategic timing. The F2P model it pioneered would later be optimized by machine learning systems for player retention and monetization, making QuizQuiz a historical inflection point for AI in game economics.

Classification

Mechanic: Quiz puzzle hybrid — trivia answers become puzzle attacks

Genre: Quiz / Competitive Puzzle

Prototype Notes

20 general knowledge trivia questions. Split-screen with 6-wide, 12-tall puzzle fields. AI opponent has variable difficulty — answers in 2-8 seconds with ~70% accuracy. Speed-based attack power rewards fast knowledge recall. Blocks settle between questions with brief animation.
