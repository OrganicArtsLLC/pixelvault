How to Play

You are Chuchel, a fuzzy orange creature obsessed with a cherry. Click objects in each scene to interact — wrong clicks produce funny animations, the right sequence advances the story.

  • Mouse — Click objects to interact
  • R — Restart current scene
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

There are 6 scenes, each a self-contained visual gag. No text — humor is entirely physical.

History

Amanita Design and Czech Animation

Chuchel (2018) was created by Jakub Dvorský and Amanita Design in Prague, Czech Republic. The studio is renowned for Machinarium (2009), Samorost (2003), and Botanicula (2012) — all wordless, visually rich point-and-click games.

The Czech Republic has a deep tradition of animated comedy, from Jan Švankmajer's surrealist films to the beloved cartoon Krtek (The Little Mole). Chuchel channels this tradition into interactive form — every click is a punchline.

The game won the Excellence in Visual Art award at the 2018 Independent Games Festival. Its design philosophy: make the player laugh, even when they fail.

AI Archaeology

Comedy as Interaction Design

Chuchel inverts the traditional adventure game failure state. Wrong clicks don't punish — they reward with comedy. This "fail-forward" design anticipates AI systems that learn from every interaction, treating mistakes as data rather than errors.

The visual gag format — self-contained scenes with cause-and-effect chains — maps directly to state machines, but the humor emerges from unexpected state transitions. Generating comedy remains one of AI's hardest unsolved problems.

Prototype Notes

This prototype captures the core loop: click objects, watch reactions, find the sequence. Six scenes recreate the cherry-chase structure with physical comedy. The fuzzy character design and bright palette honor Amanita Design's aesthetic.
