ADV-009 · BORDER CHECK

ADV — Adventure Prototype #009 ★★★★☆

How to Play

You are a border checkpoint inspector in the fictional country of Arstotzka. Examine each traveler's documents against today's rules. Approve or deny entry.

  • Mouse click — Interact with documents, stamps, and buttons
  • Click NEXT to call the next person
  • Read the Rules Board on the left for today's requirements
  • Check passport details: country, expiration, gender, photo
  • Click APPROVE (green stamp) or DENY (red stamp)
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Check everything: Country allowed? Passport expired? Gender match? Photo match?
  • Read the rules: They change every day. New restrictions are added.
  • Mistakes cost you: 3 citations in a day = game over.
  • Moral dilemmas: Some people beg for entry. Letting them in when rules say no is compassionate but risky.
  • Pay bills: After each day, choose what your family can afford — rent, food, heat.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Papers, Please (2013, Lucas Pope / 3909 LLC)

Papers, Please (2013) by Lucas Pope redefined what a game could be about. Set in a fictional Eastern Bloc country, the player works as a border inspector, checking passports and documents against an ever-changing set of rules. The mechanical act of bureaucratic inspection becomes a vehicle for moral drama — every decision to approve or deny has human consequences.

The game won the BAFTA for Strategy and Simulation and inspired a wave of "empathy games" that used systems mechanics to create emotional experiences. Its genius lies in making the player complicit in an authoritarian system through the mundane act of following rules.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 6 — Deep Learning Dawn (2010–2020)

Contemporary AI: Deep learning, convolutional neural networks, anomaly detection, facial recognition.

Hidden Intelligence: The player IS a classifier. Each document check is a feature comparison — does this input (passport) match the expected distribution (rules)? Discrepancies are anomalies. The daily rule changes shift the decision boundary, requiring the player to retrain their mental model. This is exactly how anomaly detection systems work: learn the normal pattern, flag deviations.

The Convergence: Papers, Please arrived just as automated passport control and facial recognition were being deployed at real borders. The game asks: what is lost when we automate compassion? The player's moral struggle is precisely what a machine cannot replicate.

If AI Had Designed This: Adaptive difficulty based on player error patterns. Procedural generation of document discrepancies calibrated to the player's detection rate. NPCs whose stories are generated by language models to maximize emotional impact.

Classification

Series: ADV — Adventure

Core Mechanic

Document inspection with moral weight. Compare passports against daily rules. Approve or deny. Balance accuracy against compassion. Manage family expenses with earned wages.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Document comparison — does checking details feel tense and engaging?
  • Rule escalation — do daily rule changes create escalating cognitive load?
  • Moral tension — do story moments create genuine dilemmas?
  • Resource pressure — does the family economy add stakes to accuracy?

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. Five days of escalating rules with 10 travelers per day. Procedurally generated passports with deliberate discrepancies. Moral dilemma characters appear on specific days. Family economy with rent, food, and heat expenses.
