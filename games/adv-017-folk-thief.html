History & Lineage

Ancestry: Adventures of Iljimae (1987, Aproman, Korea, MSX)

Iljimae (일지매, "One Branch Plum") is a legendary figure from Korean folklore — a masked swordsman of the Joseon dynasty who robbed corrupt nobles and distributed wealth to the poor, leaving a plum blossom at each scene. The character first appeared in a 1972 newspaper serial novel and became a cultural icon through TV dramas and films.

The 1987 MSX game by Korean developer Aproman was one of Korea's earliest original game characters. While most Korean games of the era were clones of Japanese titles, Iljimae drew from uniquely Korean folklore, making it a landmark in Korean game development history.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 2 — The Arcade Mind (1978–1985 patterns)

Contemporary AI: Rule-based systems, early pathfinding algorithms, finite state machines for enemy behavior.

Hidden Intelligence: The Robin Hood mechanic — steal then redistribute — creates a prosocial feedback loop unusual for its era. Most platformers rewarded accumulation; Iljimae rewarded giving away. This redistribution mechanic mirrors optimization problems in resource allocation.

The Convergence: Enemy patrol patterns use simple state machines (patrol, chase, return). The level designs use Korean architectural grammar — hanok rooftops, stone walls, pine forests — as procedural building blocks.

If AI Had Designed This: Dynamic difficulty based on player generosity. Adaptive guard patrol routes that learn from player patterns. Procedural Joseon-era architecture generation.
