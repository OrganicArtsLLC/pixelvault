SHT — Shooter Prototype #006 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Top-down tactical shooter. Clear rooms of enemies to advance through missions. Use cover and choose the right weapon for each engagement.

  • WASD — Move
  • Mouse — Aim
  • Click — Shoot
  • 1 — Assault Rifle (balanced)
  • 2 — Shotgun (close range, spread)
  • 3 — Sniper Rifle (long range, high damage)
  • R — Reload
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Cover: Walls block bullets — use them to break line of sight.
  • Health regen: Stay out of fire for a few seconds to regenerate health.
  • Weapon choice: Shotgun devastates at close range. Sniper picks off distant targets. Rifle is versatile.
  • Flanking: Enemies will try to flank you — watch your sides.
  • Minimap: Top-left shows the layout and enemy positions in explored areas.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Medal of Honor (1999) → Counter-Strike (2000) → Call of Duty (2003)

Call of Duty (2003) transformed the FPS genre by emphasizing scripted cinematic moments, squad-based combat, and regenerating health. The series became the defining military shooter franchise, with its multiplayer component shaping online gaming culture.

The tactical shooter sub-genre evolved from pure twitch reflexes toward positional play — cover usage, weapon selection, and coordinated movement became as important as aim.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 5 — Deep Learning

Enemy AI in modern tactical shooters represents a significant leap: soldiers seek cover when suppressed, attempt flanking maneuvers, throw grenades to flush players out, and coordinate movements. This behavior mirrors planning-based AI systems that reason about spatial relationships, opponent state, and team coordination.

The shift from "patrol → detect → chase" to "assess → position → engage" mirrors the broader AI evolution from reactive agents to deliberative ones.

Prototype Notes

Distills the tactical shooter to its core loop: advance into a room, identify threats, use cover and weapon selection to clear, then move on. Enemies use cover-seeking and flanking to create emergent tactical situations. Three weapon types create distinct engagement ranges and risk/reward tradeoffs.
