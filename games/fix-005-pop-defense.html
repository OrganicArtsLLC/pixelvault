FIX-005 · POP DEFENSE

FIX — Fixed Shooter Prototype #005 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Pop balloons (bloons) by placing towers along a winding path. Each bloon that reaches the end costs you a life. Survive 25 waves of increasingly tough bloons.

  • Click a tower in the shop panel (right side), then click the map to place it
  • Click a placed tower to upgrade it (costs increase per level)
  • Space — Start next wave (or auto-starts after delay)
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel
  • Escape — Cancel tower placement / close info

Tower Types

  • Dart Monkey ($200): Cheap, single target, short range. Your bread and butter.
  • Tack Shooter ($350): Shoots 8 projectiles in all directions. Great at chokepoints.
  • Ice Tower ($300): Slows all bloons in range. Doesn't pop, but enables other towers.
  • Sniper ($400): Infinite range, single target, high damage. Picks off tough bloons.
  • Bomb Tower ($500): Area damage, pops groups. Can't pop black bloons!
  • Super Monkey ($2500): Extremely fast fire rate, long range. The endgame tower.

Bloon Types

  • Red (1 HP) — basic bloon
  • Blue (2 HP) — releases red when popped
  • Green (3 HP) — releases blue
  • Yellow (4 HP) — releases green, faster
  • Pink (5 HP) — releases yellow, fastest
  • Black (3 HP) — immune to bombs!
  • MOAB (200 HP) — massive blimp, costs 10 lives if leaked

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Bloons TD (2007, Ninja Kiwi) ← Desktop Tower Defense (2007) ← Rampart (1990, Atari)

Tower defense as a genre crystallized from Warcraft III custom maps (2003-2004), but Bloons TD refined it into an accessible, addictive formula. Ninja Kiwi's original Bloons was a dart-throwing puzzle; the TD spin-off married it to the tower defense template and became one of the most successful Flash game franchises, surviving the Flash apocalypse to thrive on mobile.

The layered bloon system (pop a blue to reveal a red) creates emergent complexity from simple rules — a hallmark of elegant game design.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 5 — Deep Learning (2006+)

Contemporary AI: Deep reinforcement learning was just emerging. AlphaGo was still a decade away. But the facility location problem that underlies tower placement was well-studied in operations research.

Hidden Intelligence: Tower defense is a constraint satisfaction puzzle wearing real-time clothing. Place fixed agents optimally along a known path to intercept moving targets. This is equivalent to the facility location problem — NP-hard in general. The player develops greedy heuristics: place towers at curves (maximizing time-in-range), diversify damage types, create kill zones. These are the same approximation strategies OR researchers use.

The Convergence: Modern TD AI uses reinforcement learning to discover tower placements that surprise human experts — finding synergies between tower types and positions that emerge from the combinatorial explosion of possibilities.

Classification

Series: FIX (Fixed Shooter / Fixed Position) — player places stationary defenses

Core Mechanic: Spatial optimization under economic constraints

Input: Mouse-driven placement and selection

Prototype Notes

This sketch captures the essential tower defense loop: earn → place → optimize → survive. The winding path provides natural chokepoints for strategic placement. Wave pacing escalates from gentle tutorial to frantic endgame. The MOAB boss at wave 20+ tests whether your economy and placement hold up under extreme pressure.
