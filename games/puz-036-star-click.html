PUZ — Puzzle Prototype #036 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Find the hidden star on each screen. Every screen hides a star behind a different interaction — click, drag, wait, draw, shake. The joy is in discovering what each screen wants from you.

  • Mouse — Click, drag, and interact with each screen
  • N — Skip to next screen
  • R — Reset current screen
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Hints

  • Each screen has a unique puzzle — experiment!
  • Some screens respond to clicking, some to dragging
  • Some require patience, others require speed
  • Watch for visual clues about what to do

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Hoshi Saga (2007, Yoshio Ishii / Nekogames) → Flash puzzle anthology → Cursor*10 (same creator)

Yoshio Ishii, working under the name Nekogames, created Hoshi Saga as a series of Flash puzzle screens where each level hides a star behind a unique interaction. The series eventually spanned multiple volumes with dozens of screens. Ishii is also known for Cursor*10, another innovative Flash game where you cooperate with replays of your past selves. Both games exemplify the creativity of the Flash game era, where small independent developers could experiment freely with interaction design.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 5 — Generalization Wave

Hoshi Saga represents a fascinating challenge for AI: each screen requires a completely different interaction strategy. Unlike most games where a single policy suffices, this demands meta-learning — the ability to quickly hypothesize and test new interaction models. This mirrors the exploration-exploitation tradeoff in reinforcement learning, where the agent must first discover what actions are meaningful before optimizing them.

Prototype Notes

12 unique puzzle screens, each with a different interaction mechanic. Screens range from simple clicking to drawing, dragging, sequencing, and patience puzzles. The star reveal animation celebrates each discovery with a satisfying glow effect.
