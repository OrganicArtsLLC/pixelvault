HYB-009 · OKHLOS

HYB — Hybrid Prototype #009 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Movement & Recruiting

You are a philosopher leading an angry mob through ancient Greece. Move near citizens to recruit them into your mob.

  • Arrow keys — Move philosopher (leader)
  • Space or Click — Direct mob to attack nearest target
  • Walk near blue citizens to recruit them into your mob
  • Different citizen types have different abilities

Citizen Types

  • Philosophers (purple) — Boost morale; mob regenerates HP faster
  • Warriors (red) — Deal double damage when attacking
  • Slaves (brown) — Carry rubble from destroyed buildings for bonus points

Combat

  • Attack buildings to destroy them and earn points
  • Each level ends with a mythological boss: Medusa, Minotaur, Zeus
  • The mob surges toward attack targets and scatters when hit
  • If your mob shrinks to just you (the philosopher), game over

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Okhlos (2016, Coffee Powered Machine/Devolver Digital) ← Pikmin (2001, Nintendo)

Okhlos was developed by Coffee Powered Machine, a small studio based in Buenos Aires, Argentina. Published by Devolver Digital in 2016, it drew on Greek mythology and the concept of mob rule (from the Greek okhlos, meaning crowd or mob). The game asked: what if you controlled not a hero, but an entire angry mob?

The game blended action combat with crowd management. Your philosopher leader recruited citizens of different classes — warriors, philosophers, slaves — each contributing differently to the mob’s capabilities. The mob moved as a physics-driven swarm, surging forward to attack, scattering when damaged, and reforming around the leader.

As an Argentine indie game, Okhlos represented the growing Latin American game development scene. Coffee Powered Machine brought a distinctly irreverent sensibility to Greek mythology, with citizens overthrowing the gods themselves.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 6 — Deep Learning (emergent swarm behavior from simple boid rules)

Swarm Intelligence: The mob in Okhlos demonstrates emergence — complex group behavior arising from simple individual rules. Each citizen follows basic flocking principles (separation, alignment, cohesion toward the leader), yet the crowd as a whole exhibits organic surging, scattering, and reforming that feels genuinely alive.

Multi-Agent Systems: The heterogeneous mob (warriors, philosophers, slaves) prefigures multi-agent AI systems where different specialist agents collaborate. Each citizen type contributes a different capability, and the mob’s overall effectiveness depends on its composition — a concept central to modern ensemble methods in machine learning.

If AI Had Designed This: It might have given the mob emergent leadership — sub-groups forming around particularly charismatic citizens, creating a hierarchical swarm structure that adapts its formation to different combat situations.

Classification

Series: HYB — Hybrid

Games that combine two or more distinct gameplay systems. Okhlos fuses real-time action with crowd management and resource strategy.

Core Mechanic

Mob control — recruit citizens into an angry mob that moves as a physics-based swarm. The mob surges, scatters, and reforms. Storm buildings and fight mythological bosses. Mob size equals power.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Flocking/boid behavior — does the mob feel organic and alive?
  • Crowd physics — do surging and scattering feel impactful?
  • Citizen differentiation — do types feel meaningfully different?
  • Mob-as-resource — does managing mob size create strategic tension?

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. The flocking system uses separation, cohesion, and alignment forces with the philosopher as attractor. Mob members have individual positions and velocities. Three citizen types are differentiated by color and combat contribution. Three levels with escalating bosses (Medusa, Minotaur, Zeus) test mob management under pressure. Buildings are destructible targets that yield points and sometimes recruits.
