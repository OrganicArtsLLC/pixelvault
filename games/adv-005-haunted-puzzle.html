ADV-005 · HAUNTED PUZZLE

ADV — Adventure Prototype #005 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Explore the mansion with three characters. Click verbs then objects to interact. Rescue Sandy!

  • Click a verb then click an object to act
  • Use + item + object: Click Use, click inventory item, then click target
  • 1/2/3 — Switch characters | R — Restart | ? — Info

Characters

  • Dave (blue) — Strong, can push heavy objects
  • Bernard (green) — Nerd, fixes machines
  • Razor (magenta) — Musician, plays instruments

Inventory is shared. Click doors/stairs to move. Watch text feedback for clues.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Zork (1977) → Maniac Mansion (1987, Lucasfilm Games / Ron Gilbert & Gary Winnick)

First game built on the SCUMM engine, which drove adventure logic via a visible verb-noun grammar. Pioneered the "no dead ends" philosophy — every puzzle stayed solvable, unlike Sierra's frequent softlocks.

Multiple playable characters with unique abilities created emergent solution paths a decade before the concept went mainstream. SCUMM went on to power Monkey Island, Day of the Tentacle, and Sam & Max.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1978–1993)

Contemporary AI: Expert systems and frame-based reasoning. SCUMM's verb-noun grammar mirrors slot-filling in frame-based AI.

Hidden Intelligence: SCUMM abstracted adventure logic into a verb-noun state machine — each interaction is VERB(object, [target]), structurally identical to action-entity-parameter patterns in STRIPS/PDDL planners.

Convergence: Modern LLM game agents use this same verb-object-target grammar. SCUMM made the action space visible to humans — early transparent AI interaction design.

Multi-Agent: Multiple characters with unique abilities = proto-multi-agent planning, now standard in role-specialized AI agent systems.

Classification
