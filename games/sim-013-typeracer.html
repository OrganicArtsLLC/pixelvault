How to Play

Type the displayed passage as fast and accurately as you can. Your speed (WPM) and accuracy are measured in real time.

  • Start typing to begin the timer
  • Correct characters turn green
  • Wrong characters flash red — press Backspace to fix
  • Enter — start next passage after finishing
  • R — restart current passage
  • ? — toggle this info panel

Scoring

  • WPM — Words per minute (1 word = 5 characters)
  • Accuracy — Correct keystrokes out of total keystrokes
  • Ratings: Beginner (<30), Intermediate (30-59), Fast (60-89), Expert (90-119), Champion (120+)

History

Typing games trace back to Mavis Beacon Teaches Typing (1987), which gamified touch-typing education. TypeRacer (2008) turned it into a competitive multiplayer race. ZType (2011) merged typing with shoot-em-ups. Monkeytype (2020) became the modern minimalist standard for speed testing. The typing test remains the most universal measure of human text-input bandwidth.

AI Archaeology

Touch typing is the most practiced motor skill in computing. The average proficient typist manages 40-80 WPM — roughly 4-8 characters per second. Champions exceed 150 WPM. This bandwidth constraint is the fundamental bottleneck in human-computer communication. As AI systems generate text orders of magnitude faster than humans can type, the asymmetry between input and output bandwidth reshapes interaction design.
