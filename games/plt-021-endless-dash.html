How to Play

Your cookie runs automatically through a candy-themed world. Jump over gaps and spikes, slide under barriers, and collect jellies to boost your score. Watch your energy bar — it depletes over time. Grab energy potions to keep running!

  • Space — Jump (hold for higher jump)
  • ↓ — Slide under barriers
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Collectibles

  • Yellow jellies: +10 points each
  • Pink jellies: +25 points each
  • Energy potions: Restore energy

Tips

  • Hold jump: Holding space makes you jump higher — tap for short hops, hold for big leaps.
  • Slide early: Start sliding before you reach a barrier to pass safely underneath.
  • Energy management: Prioritize energy potions when your bar is low — running out ends the game.
  • Speed increases: The cookie accelerates over time, so obstacles come faster.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Cookie Run (2013, Devsisters) ← Jetpack Joyride (2011, Halfbrick) ← Canabalt (2009, Adam Saltsman)

Cookie Run (쿠키런, 2013) was created by Korean studio Devsisters and became a cultural phenomenon across Korea and Asia. Originally released as “Cookie Run for Kakao” on the KakaoTalk messaging platform, it reached over 20 million downloads within months of launch.

The game reimagined the auto-runner genre with a charming dessert theme and an innovative energy-depletion mechanic that added strategic depth to the reflex-based gameplay. Players had to balance collecting jellies for score against grabbing energy potions to survive longer. Each cookie character had unique abilities, creating a collectible meta-game.

Its success spawned Cookie Run: OvenBreak (2016) and Cookie Run: Kingdom (2021), the latter becoming a global hit that expanded the franchise into RPG territory. Devsisters grew from a small Seoul startup into a major game company, demonstrating Korea’s influence on mobile gaming innovation.

AI Era: 6 — Foundation Models (2020–present)

Contemporary AI: Large language models, multimodal AI, reinforcement learning from human feedback.

Insight: Cookie Run’s energy-depletion mechanic reframes the auto-runner from pure reflex into resource management. The player must balance risk (collecting distant jellies) against safety (staying on the safe path), mirroring the exploration-exploitation tradeoff central to reinforcement learning. The game’s character collection system also parallels how foundation models learn diverse capabilities.

Classification

  • Secondary: Resource management, reflex
  • Input: 2-button (jump + slide)

Prototype Notes

This prototype implements the core auto-running loop with jump/slide mechanics, energy depletion, jelly collection, obstacle variety (gaps, spikes, barriers), and progressive speed increase. The dessert-themed visuals use candy colors and frosting-styled platforms to evoke the original’s charming aesthetic.
