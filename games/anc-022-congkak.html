ANC-022 · CONGKAK

ANC — Ancient Games Prototype #022 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Pick up all stones from one of your pits and sow them counterclockwise around the board. Collect the most stones in your store (the large pit on your right).

  • Click a pit on your side (bottom row) or press 1-7
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Rules (Store-in-Circuit)

  • Sowing: Pick up all stones from a pit, drop one in each subsequent pit counterclockwise — including your own store.
  • Store in circuit: Unlike African mancala, your store IS part of the sowing path. This is the key difference.
  • Skip opponent's store: Never drop stones in the opponent's store.
  • Bonus turn: If your last stone lands in your store, you get another turn.
  • Capture: If your last stone lands in an empty pit on your side, capture that stone and all stones in the opposite pit into your store.
  • Game end: When one side is completely empty, remaining stones on the other side go to that side's owner's store.
  • Victory: Most stones wins. Total is 98 (7 pits x 7 stones x 2 sides).

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Congkak/Sungka (~16th century) — part of the vast Mancala family

Congkak (also spelled Congklak) is a traditional mancala game widely played in Malaysia, Singapore, Brunei, and southern Thailand. The Filipino variant is called Sungka. The name may derive from the Malay word "congkak" meaning "mental calculation."

Traditionally carved from hardwood (often mahogany or teak) in the shape of a boat or naga (dragon), Congkak boards are works of art. The game was historically associated with aristocratic women in the Malay courts, though it was played widely across social classes.

The 2x7 board with stores is the most common configuration, though 2x5 and 2x9 variants exist. The key mechanical difference from West African mancala (Oware) is that the player's store is part of the sowing circuit — stones pass through and accumulate in your store during normal play, creating a different tempo and strategy.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 2 — Knowledge Engineering (1965-1985)

Contemporary AI: The store-in-circuit mechanic creates asymmetric path lengths depending on which pit you choose. Expert systems can encode heuristics: prefer moves that land in your store (bonus turn), set up captures, and avoid feeding the opponent's side.

Hidden Intelligence: With 7 pits and 7 initial stones, the game tree is significantly larger than 6-pit Kalah. The store-in-circuit creates different positional dynamics — stones accumulate in stores naturally through sowing, not just through captures. This changes the tempo: games tend to have more bonus turns and the store fills faster.

The Convergence: Congkak's store-in-circuit is a design insight that changes the feel of mancala. By including the store in the path, every sowing action has a chance of scoring. The game becomes more flowing, less about hoarding — a different optimization landscape.

If AI Had Designed This: An AI optimizing for "satisfying micro-rewards during play" might converge on store-in-circuit. Each sow has a chance of landing in the store, providing frequent positive feedback — a proto-variable-ratio reward schedule.

Core Mechanic

Seed sowing with store-in-circuit. 2x7 board, 7 stones per pit. Counterclockwise sowing includes your store but skips opponent's. Bonus turns on store landing. Captures on empty own-pit landing.
