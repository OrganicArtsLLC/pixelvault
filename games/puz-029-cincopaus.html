PUZ-029 · CINCO PAUS

PUZ — Puzzle Prototype #029 ★★★★☆

How to Play

You are on a 7×7 grid with 5 mystery wands (varas). Each wand has 3 hidden abilities that you must discover through experimentation. All text is deliberately in Portuguese.

  • Click a wand at the bottom to select it (or press 1-5)
  • Click a direction on the grid (or use Arrow keys) to use the selected wand
  • Without a wand selected, Arrow keys move your character
  • Reach the Escadas (stairs) to descend to the next floor
  • Defeat or avoid Inimigos (enemies) — they chase you each turn
  • Each wand has 3 uses per floor — use them wisely
  • R — Restart | ? — Toggle this info panel

Wand Abilities

  • Dano — Damage enemies in the target direction
  • Teletransporte — Teleport to a random empty cell
  • Empurrar — Push enemies away from you
  • Escudo — Grant a temporary shield
  • Curar — Restore health
  • Congelar — Freeze nearby enemies for 2 turns
  • Revelar — Reveal one hidden ability of a random wand
  • Invocar — Summon a friendly decoy

The mystery IS the game — you won't know what a wand does until you try it. Subtle hint icons appear as you discover abilities.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Cinco Paus (2017, Michael Brough) ← 868-HACK (2013) ← Imbroglio (2016)

Michael Brough, working from Wellington, New Zealand, created Cinco Paus as a deliberate exercise in designed obscurity. All text is in Portuguese — not Brough's native language — forcing even English-speaking players into the same state of bewilderment. The game's title means "Five Sticks" in Portuguese. Brough's roguelikes consistently explore how minimal rulesets create deep emergent strategy. Cinco Paus pushes this further by hiding the rules themselves, making discovery the core loop.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 7 — Generative Era

Cinco Paus represents a fascinating inversion of AI-era design thinking. Where most modern games use procedural generation to create content variety, Brough uses it to create knowledge variety. Each run's wand assignments are a unique puzzle — the player must build a mental model of a system they cannot directly observe. This mirrors challenges in interpretable AI: understanding what a black-box system does through experimentation rather than inspection.

Classification

Mechanic: Mystery wand roguelike with deliberate obscurity

Genre: Roguelike / Puzzle / Experimentation

Prototype Notes

7×7 grid, 5 wands with 3 random abilities each from a pool of 8. Portuguese labels throughout. 5 floors of increasing difficulty. Abilities are hidden until discovered through use. Simplified from original — fewer enemy types, streamlined wand effects. Michael Brough, Wellington NZ, 2017.
