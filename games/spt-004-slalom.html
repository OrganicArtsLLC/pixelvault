SPT-004 · GATE RACER

SPT — Sports Prototype #004 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Ski downhill through 30 gates as fast as possible. Pass between each pair of flags (red on left, blue on right). Missing a gate adds a 5-second penalty. Hitting an obstacle crashes you for 2 seconds plus a time penalty.

  • ← → — Carve left/right (turn rate depends on speed)
  • ↑ — Tuck position (go faster, wider turns)
  • ↓ — Snowplow/brake (slow down, tighter turns)
  • R — Restart run
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Plan ahead: Look at the next gate while passing the current one.
  • Speed vs control: Tucking is fast but makes turning harder. Brake before tight gates.
  • Clean runs matter: Missing gates adds 5 seconds each — consistency beats raw speed.
  • Avoid obstacles: Trees and rocks cause a 2-second crash stun plus 3-second penalty.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Slalom (1987, Rare/Nintendo) → Ski Free (1991, Microsoft/Chris Pirih) → SSX (2000, EA)

Slalom (1987) for the NES was one of Rare's earliest console games, designed by Tim and Chris Stamper. It used a pseudo-3D perspective to simulate downhill skiing, with the player weaving between gates and dodging obstacles like trees, snowmen, and sledders.

Ski Free (1991) by Chris Pirih became a beloved Windows pack-in game. Its overhead perspective and infamous Abominable Snowman made it a cultural touchstone. Both games capture the essential tension of alpine skiing: speed versus control.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1978–1993)

Contemporary AI: Path planning algorithms (A*, Dijkstra) were well-established. Optimal trajectory computation under physics constraints was an active research area in robotics.

Hidden Intelligence: Slalom skiing is a sequential decision problem under momentum constraints. Each gate approach must account for current velocity and the position of the NEXT gate. The player is solving a look-ahead path optimization where greedy choices (shortest path to current gate) may make the next gate unreachable. This is a simplified version of the motion planning problem in robotics — nonholonomic planning with momentum.

The Convergence: Modern ski racing simulation uses the same trajectory optimization that autonomous vehicles employ. Both must plan curved paths through waypoints while respecting velocity-dependent turn constraints. The game mechanic IS the real physics.

If AI Had Designed This: Gate placement would be procedurally optimized to create maximal decision tension — each gate positioned so that the optimal approach depends on the gate after it, creating a chain of coupled decisions.
