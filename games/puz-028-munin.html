How to Play

You are Munin, Odin's raven, stripped of your wings. Rotate sections of the Norse landscape to create connected paths and collect feathers to regain flight.

  • Click a section to rotate it 90° clockwise
  • The raven walks automatically along platforms
  • Create connected paths so the raven can reach all feathers
  • R — restart the current level
  • N — next level (after solving)
  • ? — toggle this info panel

History

Munin was developed by Gojira, a small Swedish studio, released in 2014. The game draws deeply from Norse mythology — Munin is one of Odin's two ravens (the other being Huginn), representing memory. In the myth, the ravens fly across the world gathering information for Odin.

The core mechanic — rotating rectangular sections of the level — creates an elegant puzzle where the landscape itself becomes the puzzle piece. Platforms, walls, and paths shift as sections rotate, fundamentally changing the topology of each level.

The game belongs to a lineage of rotation-based puzzles including Rubik's Cube (1974) and digital descendants like Braid's time manipulation and FEZ's perspective rotation.

AI Archaeology

Classification

AI-ERA 5 (Deep Learning). Section rotation creates a combinatorial state space where spatial reasoning must account for how local rotations affect global path connectivity — a challenge for modern spatial AI.

Mechanical Lineage

Rotation puzzles → section-based level manipulation → topology-changing mechanics. Each rotation is a discrete transform that reshapes the playfield's connectivity graph.

Prototype Notes

This prototype distills Munin's section-rotation mechanic into 8 puzzles with increasing complexity. The raven auto-walks, emphasizing the pure puzzle of section rotation. Norse visual motifs (dark blues, golds, runic borders) echo the original's hand-painted Scandinavian art style.
