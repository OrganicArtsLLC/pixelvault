MAZ-003 · ICE SLIDE

MAZ — Maze Prototype #003 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Push ice blocks to crush Sno-Bees. Clear all enemies to advance to the next level. Don't let them touch you.

  • ← → ↑ ↓ — Move (one tile at a time)
  • Space — Push or break ice block in facing direction
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Sliding blocks: Pushed blocks slide until they hit a wall or another block. Anything in their path is crushed.
  • Diamond blocks: Yellow/gold blocks cannot be destroyed. Align all 3 for a bonus.
  • Wall vibration: Push against an outer wall to stun nearby enemies briefly.
  • Hatching: Some ice blocks contain eggs — enemies will hatch from them over time.
  • Break blocks: If there is no room to push (block behind), you break the ice block instead.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Pengo (1982, Sega) → Adventures of Lolo (1989) → Kickle Cubicle (1990)

Pengo (1982, Sega) was a maze game where the player pushes ice blocks to crush enemies. It introduced the "sliding projectile" mechanic — using the environment as a weapon. The penguin character and colorful ice maze made it visually distinctive. The bonus for aligning diamond blocks added a secondary objective.

The game's influence extends to Sokoban-style puzzles and block-pushing mechanics in countless games. The idea that the environment itself is your weapon — rather than bullets or swords — was a conceptual leap that resonated through puzzle-action hybrids for decades.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1978–1983)

Contemporary AI: Expert systems and pattern recognition reaching maturity. Early neural network revival stirring. Game AI still purely rule-based.

Hidden Intelligence: The sliding-block crush mechanic is a planning problem where each push has cascading spatial consequences. The player must reason about trajectories through a dynamic obstacle field, anticipating both block physics and enemy pathfinding simultaneously. This is a multi-step lookahead problem embedded in real-time action.

The Convergence: Pengo's block-pushing creates a dynamic graph where each action restructures the maze topology. The enemies must pathfind through a constantly changing environment — a problem that modern AI addresses with replanning algorithms and dynamic navigation meshes.

If AI Had Designed This: Procedurally generated maze layouts optimized for maximum "push opportunity density" — ensuring the player always has tactical options without making any single strategy dominant.

Classification

Series: MAZ — Maze

Games built around navigation of constrained space. In Pengo's case, the maze is not just a constraint but a weapon — the ice blocks are both obstacles and projectiles.

Core Mechanic

Environmental weapon via sliding blocks. Grid-based movement. Push ice blocks as sliding projectiles. Diamond block alignment bonus. Enemies pathfind through a dynamically changing maze. Wall vibration stun.
