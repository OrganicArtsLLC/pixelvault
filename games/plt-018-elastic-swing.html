PLT-018 · ELASTIC SWING

PLT — Platformer Prototype #018 ★★★★☆

How to Play

You are a pink-haired girl with a magical rubber fishing rod. Cast your line to grapple surfaces, swing with elastic momentum, and fling yourself to the exit door.

  • A / D or ← / → — Move left/right
  • Mouse — Aim the fishing rod
  • Click or Space — Cast rod / attach to surface
  • Click or Space again — Release rod (fling!)
  • W or ↑ — Reel in (shorten line)
  • S or ↓ — Let out (lengthen line)
  • R — Restart level
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Rubber Physics Tips

  • Swing: Build momentum by moving left/right while attached.
  • Stretch & Fling: Fall away from the anchor to stretch the line, then release for a massive launch.
  • Reel In: Hold Up to shorten the line and pull yourself toward the anchor.
  • Bounce: The elastic line naturally bounces — use it to reach tricky spots.
  • Fish enemies: Avoid the walking fish. Touch them and you lose a life.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Umihara Kawase (1994, TNN) ← Bionic Commando (1987, Capcom) ← Pitfall! (1982, Activision)

Umihara Kawase debuted on the Super Famicom in 1994, developed by TNN (now Success). It became a Japanese cult classic, beloved for its unprecedented rubber-band grappling physics. While Bionic Commando introduced grappling to platformers, Umihara Kawase made the line elastic — a fishing rod that stretches, contracts, and bounces like a rubber band. This created an entirely new movement vocabulary where mastery of spring physics replaced traditional jumping. The series has had several sequels and remains a speedrunner favorite.

AI Archaeology

Classification

AI-ERA 3 — Expert Systems. The rubber-band physics at the heart of Umihara Kawase are essentially a real-time spring-damper simulation — the same Hooke's Law and differential equations that modern physics engines use. Each frame solves spring force, damping, and constraint satisfaction, making this an early example of the kind of continuous physics simulation that underpins modern AI-driven soft-body and cloth simulation research.

Prototype Notes

This prototype focuses on capturing the elastic feel of the original — the springy, alive quality of the fishing rod. The rubber physics use a spring-damper model with configurable stiffness and damping. Six hand-crafted levels showcase different grappling challenges: simple swings, vertical climbs, bouncing traversals, and fish-dodging gauntlets.
