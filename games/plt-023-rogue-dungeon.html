PLT-023 · DUNGREED

PLT — Platformer Prototype #023 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Descend through five procedural dungeon rooms. Defeat all enemies in each room to unlock the exit door. Find weapons as drops. Survive the boss in room 5.

  • ← → — Move left/right
  • ↑ — Jump (press again for double jump)
  • Z — Attack with primary weapon
  • X — Dash (0.2s invincibility)
  • C — Use secondary weapon/skill
  • R — Restart run
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Weapons

  • Sword: Fast melee, moderate damage
  • Axe: Slow but powerful melee
  • Bow: Ranged projectile
  • Staff: Magic projectile, piercing

Enemies

  • Slime: Hops toward you, melee contact
  • Bat: Flies erratically, contact damage
  • Archer: Fires arrows from range
  • Knight: Shielded, dash behind to hit

Tips

  • Dash is life: The brief invincibility frames let you pass through attacks and enemies.
  • Weapon drops: Defeated enemies may drop weapons — walk over them to swap your primary.
  • Double jump: You get one extra jump in the air. Combine with dash for mobility.
  • Boss patterns: The room 5 boss has telegraphed attacks — learn the patterns and dash through them.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Dungreed (2018, Team Horay) ← Dead Cells (2018, Motion Twin) ← Rogue Legacy (2013, Cellar Door Games)

Dungreed (던그리드) was created by Team Horay, a small Korean indie studio, and released in 2018. It combined the side-scrolling action of games like Dead Cells with roguelite progression, fast dash mechanics, and a distinctive anime-influenced art style popular in the Korean indie scene.

The game stood out for its responsive combat feel, particularly the dash mechanic that provided brief invincibility frames — a design choice that turned defense into an aggressive, forward-moving action rather than passive blocking. Players could dual-wield weapons, mixing melee and ranged options for emergent combo potential.

Dungreed sits in the rich 2010s wave of roguelite platformers, alongside Dead Cells, Spelunky, and Risk of Rain, but brought a distinctly Korean indie sensibility to the genre with its food-themed healing system and cozy town hub between dungeon runs.

Prototype Notes

This prototype implements the core roguelite loop: 5 procedural rooms with enemies, weapon drops, dash combat, and a boss encounter. The dash i-frames are the central mechanic, turning defense into forward motion. Death resets the run with a new random weapon.
