ADV — Action Adventure Prototype #003 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Explore a top-down overworld, fight enemies with your sword, collect items, find the dungeon key, and defeat the boss to win.

  • Arrow keys — Move in 4 directions
  • Space — Sword attack (in facing direction)
  • Z — Use equipped item (bow shoots arrows)
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Explore everywhere: Walk to the screen edge to transition to the next screen.
  • Find items: The bow, shield, and key are hidden across the overworld. Cut bushes to find them.
  • Dungeon: Find the key to enter the dungeon. The boss awaits at the end.
  • Hearts: Defeat enemies for heart drops. Find heart containers to increase max health.
  • Shield: Once found, the shield auto-blocks projectiles from the front.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: The Legend of Zelda (1986, Nintendo/Shigeru Miyamoto) ← Hydlide (1984, T&E Soft)

The Legend of Zelda redefined what a video game could be. Miyamoto designed an open world where exploration itself was the reward. The NES cartridge included battery-backed save RAM — a first for console games — because Miyamoto knew players needed sessions, not sittings.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1978–1993)

Hidden Intelligence: The overworld is a curriculum disguised as geography. Difficulty gradients and item requirements create reward shaping through world design — decades before the term existed in reinforcement learning. Screen transitions create discrete exploration units, making the vast world cognitively manageable.

The Convergence: Modern open-world game design uses the same principles Zelda pioneered: soft gating, environmental storytelling, and emergent difficulty curves.

If AI Had Designed This: A procedural overworld generator optimizing for exploration coverage and item-gate dependency chains.

Classification

Series: ADV — Action Adventure

Core Mechanic

Top-down explore, fight, collect, progress. Screen-by-screen scrolling. Sword combat. Item-gated progression. Dungeon with boss.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Screen transitions — does discrete scrolling create a sense of place?
  • Sword combat — does melee feel responsive in top-down view?
  • Item progression — does finding bow/shield/key feel rewarding?
  • Boss fight — does pattern-based combat create satisfying challenge?

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. 4x4 overworld grid, 4-room dungeon with boss. Three collectible items. Screen-by-screen transitions. Heart-based health.
