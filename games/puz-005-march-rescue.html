How to Play

Lemmings drop from the trapdoor and walk mindlessly. Assign jobs to guide enough of them to the exit. Save the required percentage to advance.

  • 1 Climber — walks up vertical walls
  • 2 Floater — opens umbrella, survives any fall
  • 3 Bomber — explodes after 5 seconds, destroying terrain
  • 4 Blocker — stands still, redirects other lemmings
  • 5 Builder — builds a diagonal staircase upward
  • 6 Basher — digs horizontally through terrain
  • 7 Miner — digs diagonally downward through terrain
  • 8 Digger — digs straight down through terrain
  • Click a lemming to assign the selected job
  • R — Restart level
  • N — Next level (after winning)
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Timing matters: Assign jobs at the right moment — a basher started too early or late wastes a skill use.
  • Blockers redirect: Place blockers to funnel lemmings toward the exit.
  • Builders bridge gaps: Builders place 12 steps diagonally upward. Time them to span pits.
  • Bombers clear terrain: Use bombers to blast through thick walls when no other job works.
  • Floaters save lives: Assign floater before a lemming reaches a long drop.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Lemmings (1991, DMA Design) → Oh No! More Lemmings (1991) → Lemmings 2: The Tribes (1993)

Lemmings (1991) by DMA Design (later Rockstar North) is one of the most influential puzzle games ever made. Inspired by a pixel animation of tiny creatures walking off cliffs, the game tasks players with saving mindless walkers by assigning them specialized jobs. Each lemming is an autonomous agent that walks, turns at walls, and falls — the player acts as a behavior programmer, modifying individual agents to solve each level.

The game sold millions across virtually every platform of its era and spawned numerous sequels and clones. Its designer, David Jones, went on to create Grand Theft Auto.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 4 — Statistical Learning (emergent behavior from simple agent rules)

Contemporary AI: Multi-agent systems research was exploring emergent behavior from simple rules — flocking (Boids, 1986), cellular automata, artificial life.

Hidden Intelligence: Each lemming is a simple finite-state automaton — walk, turn at walls, fall, die. But the player modifies their state machines in real-time by assigning jobs. This is one of the earliest mainstream examples of agent programming as gameplay. The emergent complexity comes not from individual lemming intelligence but from interactions between multiple agents and destructible terrain.

The Convergence: Lemmings anticipates modern multi-agent reinforcement learning, where simple agents with modified reward functions can collectively solve complex problems. The "assign a job" mechanic is essentially reward shaping — changing an agent's objective function.

If AI Had Designed This: An AI level designer could use evolutionary algorithms to generate levels with optimal difficulty curves — finding terrain configurations where exactly one job-assignment sequence works, maximizing the "aha moment."

Classification

Series: PUZ — Puzzle

Lemmings is a real-time puzzle game where the challenge is spatial reasoning and timing under pressure. Each level is a puzzle with limited resources (job counts) and a required save percentage.

Core Mechanic

Agent behavior programming on destructible terrain. Mindless walkers. Eight assignable jobs. Pixel-destructible terrain. Save percentage goals. Limited job counts per level.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Agent autonomy — do lemmings feel alive and mindless simultaneously?
  • Job assignment — does clicking to assign jobs feel responsive and clear?
  • Destructible terrain — do diggers, bashers, and miners carve satisfyingly?
  • Emergent solutions — can players find creative job combinations?
