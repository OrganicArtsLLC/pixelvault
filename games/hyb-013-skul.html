HYB-013 · SKULL RUN

HYB — Hybrid Prototype #013 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Movement

  • ← → — Move left/right
  • ↑ — Jump
  • Space — Attack (style depends on current skull)
  • Z — Special ability (cooldown-based)
  • Tab — Swap to your other skull

Skull System

  • You carry two skulls at once — swap between them with Tab
  • Warrior (green) — sword melee, strong vs groups
  • Mage (blue) — fireball ranged attack
  • Archer (yellow) — rapid-fire arrows
  • Berserker (red) — slow but devastating hits
  • Find new skulls between rooms — pick your loadout wisely

Rooms & Boss

  • Clear 5 procedural combat rooms, then face the boss
  • Enemies: knights (melee), archers (ranged), mages (magic)
  • Swap skulls mid-combat for tactical advantage

History & Context

Skul: The Hero Slayer (스컬, 2021) was developed by SouthPAW Games, a small Korean indie studio. The game inverts the typical fantasy narrative — you play as a tiny skeleton fighting against the “heroes” who have invaded the Demon King’s castle. Its skull-swapping mechanic, where each skull completely changes your moveset and class, creates a roguelite where adaptability and loadout synergy matter more than mastering a single playstyle.

The game was a breakout success from the Korean indie scene, which has produced several globally acclaimed roguelites. SouthPAW Games drew inspiration from Dead Cells and classic action-platformers but added the identity-swapping twist that makes each run feel distinct.

AI Archaeology

The skull-swapping mechanic is a real-time version of job/class systems found in RPGs. By making class-switching instantaneous and mid-combat, Skul creates emergent tactical depth — the AI of enemy encounters is designed so that no single skull type is optimal for every situation, forcing constant adaptation. This mirrors modern AI ensemble methods where different specialized models are selected based on the problem at hand.

Prototype Notes

This prototype focuses on the skull-swapping combat loop. Four skull types with distinct attack patterns. Procedurally generated rooms with escalating difficulty. The boss encounter tests the player’s ability to swap skulls tactically under pressure.
