How to Play

Race all 4 of your pieces around the board before the AI does. Press Space to throw the yut sticks, then click a piece to move it.

  • Throw: Press Space to toss 4 sticks. Each stick lands flat or round side up.
  • Results: Do (1 flat) = 1 step, Gae (2 flat) = 2, Geol (3 flat) = 3, Yut (4 flat) = 4 + bonus throw, Mo (0 flat/all round) = 5 + bonus throw.
  • Move: Click a piece to advance it. Click "NEW" to enter a new piece onto the board.
  • Capture: Land on an opponent's piece to send it home. You get a bonus throw!
  • Stacking: Land on your own piece to stack them — they move together as one.
  • Shortcuts: Land exactly on a corner station to take the diagonal shortcut on your next move.
  • Winning: Move all 4 pieces past the finish to win.

History

Yut Nori (윷놀이) is a traditional Korean board game dating back roughly 2,000 years to the Three Kingdoms period. It is one of Korea's most beloved folk games, traditionally played during Seollal (Korean New Year) and Chuseok (harvest festival).

The game uses four wooden sticks (yut) that are flat on one side and round on the other, serving as a binary random number generator predating six-sided dice. The board represents a cosmological map — the outer path is the earthly journey, and the diagonal shortcuts through the center represent celestial paths.

Unlike many ancient race games, Yut Nori emphasizes group play and strategy through stacking and capture mechanics. The bonus throw system for Yut and Mo results creates dramatic momentum swings.

AI Archaeology

Classification

AI Era 1 — Symbolic AI. Yut Nori is a stochastic race game with imperfect foresight. The AI uses expectiminimax evaluation — weighing each possible stick outcome by its probability when choosing which piece to move. The stacking mechanic adds a risk-aggregation dimension: grouping pieces is faster but exposes more to capture.

Prototype Notes

This prototype implements the full 29-station board (20 outer + 9 inner diagonal stations) with animated stick throwing. The AI evaluates moves based on progress, capture opportunity, and vulnerability. Stacking and diagonal shortcuts are fully functional.
