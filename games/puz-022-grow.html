PUZ-022 · GROW

PUZ — Puzzle Prototype #022 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Click the items around the border to place them on the planet one at a time. Each item interacts with previously placed items, causing them to level up. The goal is to find the one perfect order that maxes all 8 items to level 5.

  • Click an item around the border to place it
  • Watch items grow and interact on the planet
  • Items level up based on what was placed before them
  • Only 1 of 40,320 possible orders is perfect
  • R — Restart and try a new order
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Pay attention to which items boost which — the interaction hints are shown
  • If an item stays at a low level, it probably needed to be placed later
  • Items placed early get boosted by more subsequent items

History & Lineage

Ancestry: GROW (2002, On Nakayama / Eyezmaze) → GROW RPG (2004) → GROW Cube (2005)

On Nakayama created GROW in 2002 as a Flash game on his Eyezmaze website. The concept was deceptively simple: place items in order and watch them interact. The series became a beloved web game phenomenon, spawning numerous sequels (GROW Cube, GROW RPG, GROW Island) each with unique themes but the same core mechanic of finding the perfect sequence through experimentation.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 5 — Ubiquitous Intelligence

GROW represents a combinatorial optimization problem where the search space (8! = 40,320 permutations) is small enough for exhaustive search but large enough that humans must rely on pattern recognition and systems thinking. The interaction matrix creates a directed dependency graph — understanding which items catalyze which reveals the solution structure.

Classification

Mechanic: Combinatorial sequence — place items in order and watch them interact and level up

Genre: Puzzle / Experimentation

Prototype Notes

8 abstract elements with a carefully designed interaction matrix. One perfect solution that maxes all items to level 5. Visual feedback through item growth animations and level-up effects. The planet grows more vibrant as items reach higher levels.
