SPT — Sports Prototype #017 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Launch a person through the air and bounce off characters to maximize distance!

  • Click — Set launch angle (swinging indicator)
  • Click — Set launch power (filling bar)
  • Click — Aerial boost during flight (3 uses)
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Boost characters (green) bounce you upward. Block characters (red) stop you dead. Special characters (gold) give a mega launch. Time your aerial boosts to extend combos!

History

Nanaca Crash (2005) was an anonymous Japanese Flash game that went viral worldwide. A girl on a bicycle crashes into a boy, launching him across a field full of anime characters who either boost or block his flight. The game distilled the launch-and-bounce genre to its purest form.

It belonged to a wave of viral Flash launch games including Yeti Sports (2004), Kitten Cannon (2005), and later Learn to Fly (2009) and Burrito Bison (2011). The genre's appeal is primal: big number go up. The combination of skill (launch timing) and luck (character placement) creates a slot-machine-like compulsion loop.

AI Archaeology

Era 5 — Expert Systems

The optimization problem in Nanaca Crash is deceptively complex. Launch angle and power create an initial trajectory, but the random placement of field characters means optimal strategy changes every attempt. A perfect launch into an empty field scores less than a mediocre launch into a chain of boost characters.

An AI agent could learn optimal launch parameters for expected value, but the high variance from random placement means even optimal play produces wildly different results. This randomness is a feature — it keeps players coming back, convinced the next attempt will chain perfectly.
