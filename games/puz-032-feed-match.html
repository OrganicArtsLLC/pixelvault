PUZ-032 · FEED MATCH

PUZ — Puzzle Prototype #032 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Pairs of animal and food blocks fall into the well. When an animal lands adjacent to its matching food, it eats all connected matching food blocks — then disappears. Chain reactions score big points!

  • ← → or A D — Move pair left/right
  • ↑ or W — Rotate pair
  • ↓ or S — Soft drop
  • Space — Hard drop
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Animal-Food Pairs

  • Dog (brown) eats Bones
  • Rabbit (white) eats Carrots (orange)
  • Panda (black/white) eats Bamboo (green)
  • Monkey (gold) eats Bananas (yellow)

Scoring

  • Each food block eaten = 10 points
  • Chain multiplier: x2, x4, x8 for cascades
  • Cleared blocks send garbage to the AI opponent
  • Game speeds up every 500 points

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Baku Baku Animal (1995, Sega) → Puyo Puyo (1991, Compile) → Columns (1990, Sega)

Baku Baku Animal was developed by Sega for arcades and the Sega Saturn in 1995. The name comes from the Japanese onomatopoeia "baku baku" — the sound of chomping or eating. It distinguished itself from the crowded falling-block puzzle genre by replacing abstract color matching with animal-food relationships, adding a charming semantic layer. The feeding animations gave the game personality that pure abstract puzzlers lacked.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 4 — Statistical Learning

The animal-food pairing mechanic transforms the standard falling-block AI problem. Instead of optimizing for color adjacency, the AI must reason about typed relationships — an animal block is useless without its matching food nearby. This creates an asymmetric search space where placement strategy depends on block identity, not just position. Modern puzzle AI would use Monte Carlo tree search to evaluate cascade potential.

Classification

Mechanic: Animal-food matching with falling pairs and cascade clears

Genre: Falling Block Puzzle / Competitive

Prototype Notes

6-wide by 12-tall playfield for both player and AI. Pairs fall like Columns/Puyo — two blocks that can be rotated. Animals consume adjacent matching food via flood fill, then disappear. AI opponent uses simple heuristic placement. Garbage blocks rise from the bottom when opponent clears chains.
