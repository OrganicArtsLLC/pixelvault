RPG-003 · GAUNTLET

RPG — Role-Playing Game Prototype #003 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Choose a class, then fight through monster-filled dungeons. Destroy generators before they overwhelm you with enemies. Find food to survive, keys to open doors, and the exit staircase to descend deeper.

  • 1-4 — Select class (Warrior / Valkyrie / Wizard / Elf)
  • ↑ ↓ ← → — Move
  • Space — Shoot / Attack
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Classes

  • Warrior (1): Melee axe. High armor, high damage, slow. Can destroy walls.
  • Valkyrie (2): Balanced sword. Good armor, moderate damage, moderate speed.
  • Wizard (3): Ranged magic. Low HP, powerful projectile, slow movement.
  • Elf (4): Fast ranged arrows. Low armor, quick movement, rapid fire.

Tips

  • Prioritize generators: Kill spawners first or you will be overrun.
  • Health drains: Your health constantly ticks down. Keep moving forward.
  • Food heals: Turkey restores health. Do not waste it.
  • Potions: Clear all visible enemies — save for emergencies.
  • Keys: Collect keys before approaching locked doors.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Gauntlet (1985, Ed Logg, Atari) ← Dandy (1983, John Palevich) ← Dungeons & Dragons (1974)

Ed Logg designed Gauntlet at Atari in 1985, inspired by John Palevich's Dandy (1983) on the Atari 800. Gauntlet was one of the first arcade games to support four simultaneous players, each with a distinct class. The famous "Warrior needs food badly!" voice callouts became iconic.

The coin-eating model was revolutionary: health constantly drained, and inserting more quarters added health. This created an economic pressure loop that was both brilliant game design and brilliant business. Players collectively spent more because the game never truly ended — it just got harder.

Dandy, the predecessor, was a student project that pioneered the multi-player dungeon crawl format. Palevich created it as a PDP-11 game before porting to the Atari 800. Atari hired Logg to create Gauntlet after seeing Dandy's popularity.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1980–1987)

Contemporary AI: Expert systems peak, early connectionism revival. Multi-agent coordination was a research frontier.

Hidden Intelligence: Gauntlet's monster generators create a producer-consumer dynamic. Spawners produce threats faster than one player can eliminate them, forcing strategic prioritization: destroy the source, not the symptoms. This is the same resource-pressure model that appears in modern multi-agent swarm simulations and economic modeling.

Swarm Behavior: Individual enemies are simple (move toward player, attack), but in aggregate they create emergent swarm dynamics. Ghosts phase through walls, grunts bottleneck in corridors, demons provide ranged pressure. The combination creates tactical depth from simple individual rules — a core principle of swarm intelligence.

If AI Had Designed This: Dynamic difficulty adjustment via generator spawn rates, adaptive enemy routing to exploit player weaknesses, and procedural dungeon generation optimized for "flow state" maintenance. The constant health drain would be calibrated per-player to maximize coin insertion rate.

Classification

Series: RPG — Role-Playing Game

Class-based character selection with stat differentiation. Dungeon exploration with combat, items, and progression through increasingly difficult levels.

Core Mechanic

Top-down dungeon crawl with monster generators. Class selection determines playstyle. Generators create constant enemy pressure. Health-as-timer forces forward movement. Keys gate exploration. Food sustains survival.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Generator pressure — does the spawner mechanic create meaningful tactical decisions?
  • Class differentiation — do the four classes feel distinct in solo play?
  • Health drain pacing — does the constant drain create urgency without frustration?
  • Dungeon generation — do procedural rooms feel explorable and interesting?

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. Procedurally generated dungeons with rooms and corridors. Four enemy types (Ghost, Grunt, Demon, Sorcerer). Four classes with distinct stats and attack styles. Monster generators that spawn until destroyed. Food, keys, potions, treasure pickups. Exit staircase advances levels.
