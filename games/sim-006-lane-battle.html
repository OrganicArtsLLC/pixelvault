SIM-006 · LANE BATTLE

Lane-based real-time card battler with elixir economy

How to Play

  • Click a card in your hand (bottom bar) to select it
  • Click on your side of the arena (below the river) to deploy
  • Cards cost elixir (purple bar) which regenerates over time
  • Destroy enemy towers to win — lane towers must fall before the king tower
  • Match lasts 3 minutes — double elixir in the last minute
  • Most tower destruction wins; sudden death if tied

Card Types

  • Knight (4) — Melee tank, good HP
  • Archer (3) — Ranged attacker, moderate damage
  • Giant (5) — High HP, only targets buildings
  • Goblin (2) — Fast swarm of 3, low HP
  • Fireball (4) — Area damage spell
  • Musketeer (4) — Long-range single target
  • Mini PEKKA (4) — High damage melee
  • Skel Army (3) — Swarm of skeletons

History

Clash Royale (2016) by Supercell distilled the MOBA genre into real-time 3-minute card battles. It drew from tower defense, collectible card games, and the MOBA lane structure to create a new hybrid genre. Its ancestry traces through Defense of the Ancients (2003) and Herzog Zwei (1989), the proto-RTS that first combined direct control with autonomous units.

AI Archaeology

ERA 6 Transformer Era

Clash Royale compressed the MOBA formula into 3-minute matches by making units autonomous. The player's only decisions are what to deploy, where, and when — reducing real-time strategy to pure resource management and timing. This abstraction made deep strategy accessible on mobile.

Prototype Notes

MOBA meets card game in miniature. Elixir economy forces constant trade-off between defense and offense, patience and aggression. Eight card types provide enough variety for emergent strategy while keeping the cognitive load manageable for a 3-minute match.
