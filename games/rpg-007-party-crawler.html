RPG-007 · PARTY CRAWLER

RPG — Role-Playing Prototype #007 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Lead a party of 4 adventurers through a multi-level dungeon. Move in real-time on a grid, fight monsters, collect items, and find the exit stairs on each level.

  • W / ↑ — Move forward
  • S / ↓ — Move backward
  • A — Strafe left
  • D — Strafe right
  • Q — Turn left
  • E — Turn right
  • 1234 — Select party member
  • Space — Attack with selected member
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Explore: The minimap reveals tiles you have visited. Find keys to open locked doors.
  • Combat: Enemies approach in real-time. Attack before they reach you. Each party member has a cooldown after attacking.
  • Items: Walk over swords, shields, and potions to auto-collect. Potions heal the selected character.
  • Stairs: Find the stairs down to descend deeper. Clear all 4 levels to win.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Dungeon Master (1987, FTL Games) → Eye of the Beholder (1991) → Legend of Grimrock (2012)

Dungeon Master (1987) by FTL Games for the Atari ST revolutionized dungeon crawlers by introducing real-time gameplay to a genre previously dominated by turn-based systems like Wizardry and The Bard's Tale. Players controlled a party of four characters through a grid-based dungeon with pseudo-3D first-person perspective.

The game's innovations included a skill-based character system (characters improved by using abilities, not by allocating points), real-time combat requiring tactical positioning, and an intricate puzzle system involving pressure plates, switches, and timed sequences. Its mouse-driven interface was groundbreaking for the era.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1980-1993)

Contemporary AI: Expert systems at their peak. Early neural network resurgence with backpropagation (1986). Real-time systems beginning to emerge in robotics.

Hidden Intelligence: Dungeon Master's shift from turn-based to real-time mirrors the AI transition from deliberative planning to reactive architectures. The subsumption architecture (Brooks, 1986) argued that intelligence emerges from real-time interaction with the environment — exactly what Dungeon Master demanded of players. Each combat encounter becomes a reactive control problem: manage four characters' cooldowns, positions, and resources simultaneously under time pressure.

The Convergence: The grid-based world is a discrete state space, but real-time mechanics add a continuous temporal dimension. This hybrid discrete-continuous problem space anticipates modern game AI challenges. The dungeon itself is a partially observable environment where the player builds a mental SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) model.

If AI Had Designed This: An adaptive difficulty system that modulates enemy spawn rates and item placement based on party health and resource levels — maintaining optimal challenge through dynamic difficulty adjustment, a technique not formalized until the 2000s.

Classification

Series: RPG — Role-Playing

Dungeon Master sits at the intersection of RPG party management and real-time action. Grid-based movement preserves tactical depth while real-time combat adds urgency.

Core Mechanic

Real-time first-person grid crawl with party management. Four characters with individual stats and cooldowns. Grid movement with 90-degree turning. Pseudo-3D viewport with flat-shaded walls. Items, keys, locked doors, multiple dungeon levels.

What This Prototype Tests

  • First-person grid rendering — do wall segments create a convincing 3D illusion?
  • Real-time pressure — does simultaneous party management under time pressure create meaningful decisions?
  • Exploration reward — does the minimap and item discovery drive continued exploration?
  • Multi-level progression — does descending deeper feel like meaningful progress?

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. Simplified first-person rendering using pre-drawn wall segments (not raycasting). 4 dungeon levels with procedurally placed enemies and items. Party of 4 with basic stats. Real-time enemy movement and combat with cooldowns.
