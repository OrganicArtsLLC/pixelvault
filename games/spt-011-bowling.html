How to Play

Roll a bowling ball down the lane to knock down all 10 pins!

  • ← → — Aim left / right
  • Space — Hold to set power, release to throw
  • A D — Add left / right spin before throwing
  • R — Start a new game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Score spares (all pins in two throws) and strikes (all pins in one throw) for bonus points. A perfect game is 300.

History

Bowling artifacts were found in an Egyptian child's tomb dating to approximately 3200 BCE. The sport evolved through medieval European nine-pin games (documented in 1300s Germany) before arriving in America, where the modern 10-pin triangle formation was standardized around 1841 in New York — legend says to circumvent a ban on nine-pin gambling.

The American Bowling Congress (1895) standardized lane dimensions: 60 feet from foul line to head pin, 41.5 inches wide. The scoring system — where spares add the next throw and strikes add the next two — creates cascading bonus opportunities that reward consistency.

AI Archaeology

Era 3 — Knowledge Explosion

Pin scatter is a chain-reaction physics problem. The ball strikes the lead pin, which collides with neighboring pins, which hit further pins in a cascade. A perfect strike requires hitting the 1-3 pocket (for right-handers) at approximately 6 degrees. The pin action is deterministic physics that feels chaotic — the same seeming randomness that emerges from simple physical rules.

Modern bowling analysis uses computer vision to track ball speed, rev rate, and entry angle. Professional bowlers optimize for a specific ball path that creates maximum pin carry — the physics equivalent of finding the optimal input to a complex dynamical system.

Prototype Notes

This sketch implements simplified 2D pin physics with circle-circle collision response. Pin scatter uses impulse-based resolution with friction and restitution. The perspective foreshortening gives depth to the lane view. Scoring follows official 10-frame rules including the 10th frame bonus throws.
