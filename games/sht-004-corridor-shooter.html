SHT-004 · CORRIDOR SHOOTER

SHT — Shooter Prototype #004 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Navigate corridors in first-person. Find colored keys to open locked doors. Shoot enemies. Reach the exit to win.

  • W / ↑ — Move forward
  • S / ↓ — Move backward
  • A — Strafe left
  • D — Strafe right
  • ← → — Turn left / right
  • Space — Shoot
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Keys: Red and blue keys open matching doors. Check the HUD for collected keys.
  • Shotgun: Found in the level — deals more damage with spread shots.
  • Health: Green crosses restore 25 HP. Don't let health reach zero.
  • Minimap: The top-left minimap shows your position and explored areas.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Wolfenstein 3D (1992, id Software) → Doom (1993) → Quake (1996)

Doom (1993) revolutionized gaming with its fast-paced first-person combat, non-orthogonal level design, and networked multiplayer. While not true 3D (it used a BSP-based raycasting engine with height tricks), it created the illusion convincingly enough to spawn an entire genre.

The key-locked door mechanic forced exploration over pure combat, creating a loop: explore → find key → backtrack → unlock → explore deeper. This progression structure influenced level design for decades.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1980–1993)

Contemporary AI: Neural networks resurging, early machine learning. Expert systems at peak then declining.

Hidden Intelligence: Doom's raycasting is dimensional reduction — projecting a 2D grid into a 1D scanline of wall heights. Each column of pixels comes from one ray. This is the same principle behind PCA and dimensionality reduction in ML: solve a simpler problem that preserves the essential structure of the harder one.

The Convergence: Enemy AI in Doom used state machines (idle, chase, attack, pain, death) with sound-based alerting. When one enemy heard combat, nearby enemies activated — a primitive but effective information propagation network, presaging influence maximization in social networks.

If AI Had Designed This: Procedural level generation using graph grammars to ensure key-lock dependencies form a proper DAG. Enemy placement optimized via playtesting simulations to maximize engagement without frustration.

Classification

Series: SHT — Shooter

Doom transforms the shooter from a 2D reflex test into a spatial navigation puzzle with combat. The first-person perspective and key-locked progression add exploration and planning to the shooting core.

Core Mechanic

First-person raycasting with key-locked progression. Navigate grid-based corridors. Cast rays to render pseudo-3D walls. Find keys to unlock doors. Shoot enemies with hitscan weapons. Manage health and ammo resources.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Raycasting — does the pseudo-3D rendering create spatial awareness?
  • Key progression — does finding keys and unlocking doors feel rewarding?
  • Combat — do enemy encounters create tension in confined spaces?
  • Exploration — does the minimap and level layout encourage exploration?

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. Flat-colored walls with distance shading (no texture mapping). Grid-based map with raycasting renderer. One playable level with key-locked doors, enemies, pickups, and an exit.
