How to Play

Putt the ball into the hole in as few strokes as possible. Click on the ball, drag away to aim and set power, then release to putt.

  • Click + Drag on ball — Aim and set power (ball launches opposite to drag direction)
  • R — Restart current hole
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Drag distance = power: A longer drag means a harder putt.
  • Wall bounces: Use bank shots off walls to reach tricky holes.
  • Water hazards: Landing in water costs +1 stroke and resets the ball.
  • Speed matters: The ball must be rolling slowly to drop into the hole.
  • Windmill timing: Wait for the barrier to rotate out of the way.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Zany Golf (1988, Electronic Arts) → Mini-Putt (various 1980s+)

Miniature golf video games translate the real-world putting experience into a physics sandbox. Zany Golf on the Apple IIGS (1988) was a landmark — it combined top-down putting with animated obstacles like windmills, fans, and bouncing bumpers. The genre proved that simple physics (velocity, friction, reflection) could produce endlessly varied puzzles.

The drag-to-aim mechanic became the gold standard for mobile golf games, later perfected by Desert Golfing (2014) and What the Golf (2019).

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1978–1993)

Contemporary AI: Physics simulation was computationally expensive. Real-time Newtonian mechanics in games required careful approximation — Euler integration, simplified collision detection.

Hidden Intelligence: Mini golf is a trajectory planning problem with imperfect execution. The player must simulate ball physics mentally, accounting for reflections and friction. Each shot is a hypothesis about the physical world that is immediately tested. This predict-then-observe loop mirrors model-based reinforcement learning. The player builds an internal physics model and refines it hole by hole.

The Convergence: Modern robotics uses the same predict-simulate-execute loop for manipulation tasks. A robot planning a billiards shot faces the identical mathematical problem: trajectory under friction with elastic reflections off boundaries.

If AI Had Designed This: Optimal putting solutions via trajectory optimization — computing the launch angle and velocity that minimizes bounces and guarantees the ball reaches the hole below the capture speed threshold.
