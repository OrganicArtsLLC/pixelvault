PLT — Platformer Prototype #004 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Explore an interconnected alien world. Find abilities to unlock new paths. Defeat the boss in the deepest chamber.

  • ← → — Move
  • ↑ — Aim up
  • ↓ — Morph Ball (when acquired)
  • Space — Jump
  • Z — Shoot (Shift+Z for missiles)
  • R — Restart
  • ? — This panel

Tips

  • Blue doors open with any shot.
  • Red doors require missiles.
  • Green passages require Morph Ball.
  • High Jump is behind a red door.
  • Minimap (top-right) shows explored rooms.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Metroid (1986) → Super Metroid (1994) → Hollow Knight (2017)

Metroid broke the platformer mold by removing linearity. The world was a connected graph where progress required abilities that unlocked new paths. This design — now called “Metroidvania” — made the map itself the puzzle.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion

Metroid’s world is a dependency graph disguised as geography. Abilities are variables, locked paths are constraints, and the player solves a topological sort through exploration. Modern procedural Metroidvania generation is automated constraint satisfaction — the 1986 designers did this by hand.

Classification

Series: PLT — Platformer

Ability-gated exploration. Non-linear room-based world. Acquire abilities to unlock previously inaccessible paths.

Prototype Notes

Sketch rated 4/5. Interconnected room map with 3 abilities, color-coded doors, enemy types, and a boss.
