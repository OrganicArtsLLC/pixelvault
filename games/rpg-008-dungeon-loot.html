RPG-008 · DUNGEON LOOT

RPG — Role-Playing Game Prototype #008 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Descend through procedurally generated dungeon floors, slaying monsters and collecting loot. Reach the final boss to win.

  • Click — Move to location / Attack enemy
  • WASD — Move
  • Space — Attack nearest enemy
  • 1 — Use health potion
  • 2 — Use mana potion
  • R — Restart
  • ? — This panel

History

Diablo (1997) by Blizzard North fused the procedural dungeon generation of Rogue (1980) with real-time combat and randomized loot. David Brevik's original vision was a turn-based RPG; the shift to real-time action created an entirely new genre. The game's 16 randomly generated dungeon levels beneath Tristram Cathedral kept players returning for "just one more run."

AI Archaeology

Era 4 — Statistical Learning. Diablo's procedural generation uses random room placement with connectivity validation. Its loot system employs weighted probability tables with prefix/suffix modifiers, creating a combinatorial explosion of item variety from compact data. Enemy AI follows simple state machines (idle, chase, attack, flee) with per-type behavior parameters.

Prototype Notes

Distills the Diablo loop to its essence: procedural dungeon, click-to-move/attack, loot drops, and character progression. Five dungeon floors with a boss every three floors. Equipment affects stats visually in the HUD. Fog of war reveals tiles as the player explores.
