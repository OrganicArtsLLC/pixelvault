ADV-002 · CROWN ADVENTURE

ADV — Adventure Prototype #002 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Explore a fairy-tale kingdom of 10 rooms. Collect items, solve puzzles, and retrieve the crown from the Throne Room to win.

  • Arrow keys — Move the knight
  • Space — Pick up items / interact with puzzle objects
  • I — Toggle inventory display
  • 1-5 — Select active item from inventory
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Walk to screen edges to travel between rooms.
  • Stand near objects and press Space to interact.
  • Select the right item before interacting with puzzle objects.
  • Watch your lives: water, guards, and dragon fire are dangerous.
  • The map: 3×3 grid plus a bonus room behind the dragon.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Adventure (1980, Atari/Warren Robinett) → King's Quest (1984, Sierra/Roberta Williams)

King's Quest was created by Roberta Williams at Sierra On-Line and is widely regarded as the first graphical adventure game with animated characters. It replaced text parsers with a character walking through illustrated scenes — the player's avatar became part of the picture.

Warren Robinett's Adventure (1980) for the Atari 2600 pioneered the concept of room-based exploration with inventory items, but King's Quest added narrative depth, puzzle chains, and the idea that the world itself tells a story through its scenery.

The "use item X on object Y" puzzle paradigm that King's Quest popularized became the backbone of the entire adventure game genre through the 1990s.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1978–1983)

Contemporary AI: Expert systems were booming. MYCIN diagnosed diseases using if-then rules. Adventure games used the same pattern: if player has key AND is at locked door, THEN unlock door.

Hidden Intelligence: King's Quest transformed text adventure state machines into spatial puzzles. The dependency graph of items and locks became a physical world the player walks through. This is constraint satisfaction made tangible — the search space is literal geography. Each puzzle is a precondition check: do you have item X? Are you at location Y? The entire game is a directed acyclic graph of dependencies disguised as a fairy tale.

The Convergence: Modern game AI uses the same dependency-graph planning that adventure games pioneered. GOAP (Goal-Oriented Action Planning) in games like F.E.A.R. is essentially an automated adventure game puzzle solver — find the sequence of actions that satisfies all preconditions to reach the goal state.

If AI Had Designed This: An automated planner would generate the optimal item collection route — solving the constraint satisfaction problem in minimum steps. The human designer instead optimized for narrative surprise and exploration joy.

Classification

Series: ADV — Adventure

Room exploration with inventory puzzles. The player navigates a world, collects objects, and uses them to overcome obstacles — the core loop of every adventure game from Colossal Cave to modern point-and-clicks.

Core Mechanic

Room exploration with inventory-based puzzle solving. Walk through scenes, collect items, combine items with world objects to progress. Dependency chain of puzzles gates access to the final goal.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Room transitions — does walking to screen edges feel natural?
  • Item pickup — is the proximity interaction intuitive?
  • Puzzle feedback — does the player understand what to use where?
  • Exploration motivation — do distinct rooms encourage wandering?

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. 10 rooms in a 3×3+1 grid. 5 collectible items. 4 inventory puzzles chained in a dependency graph. 3 lives system. Canvas-drawn fairy-tale scenes with distinct color palettes per room.
