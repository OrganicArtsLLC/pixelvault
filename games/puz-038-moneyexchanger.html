PUZ-038 · MONEY EXCHANGER

PUZ — Puzzle Prototype #038 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Coins fall in pairs into a 6-column grid. Combine matching coins to exchange them into higher denominations, chaining all the way from 1¥ to 500¥!

  • ← → — Move falling pair left/right
  • ↓ — Soft drop (faster fall)
  • Space — Hard drop (instant)
  • Z — Rotate pair
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Exchange Rules

  • 5 × 1¥ → 1 × 5¥
  • 2 × 5¥ → 1 × 10¥
  • 5 × 10¥ → 1 × 50¥
  • 2 × 50¥ → 1 × 100¥
  • 5 × 100¥ → 1 × 500¥
  • 500¥ coins are cleared for big points!

Tips

  • Group same-denomination coins together for exchanges
  • Chain reactions cascade — a 1¥ exchange can trigger 5¥, 10¥, and beyond
  • The AI opponent gets garbage coins when you chain exchanges
  • Game ends when coins stack above the top of the grid

History

Money Idol Exchanger (1997) was developed by Face for arcade and Neo Geo hardware. Released in Japan, it used real yen denominations as its puzzle mechanic — making the exchange rules instantly understandable to players familiar with Japanese currency. The game combined falling-block puzzle mechanics with arithmetic in a way that felt natural rather than educational.

Face was a small Japanese developer known for quirky puzzle games. Money Idol Exchanger stood out because its core mechanic — currency exchange — was both novel and immediately intuitive. The satisfaction of chaining exchanges from small coins to large ones created memorable moments that pure color-matching puzzles couldn't replicate.

AI Archaeology

ERA 4 — Statistical Learning. The AI opponent evaluates board state using pattern recognition: counting adjacent same-denomination coins, estimating chain potential, and choosing placements that maximize exchange probability. This is a constraint satisfaction problem where the "constraints" are arithmetic exchange rules rather than spatial fitting.

The chain evaluation — predicting which placements lead to cascading exchanges — is a tree search problem similar to move evaluation in board games, but with the added complexity of gravity and new pieces arriving.

Prototype Notes

This prototype captures the core exchange mechanic with a vs-AI mode. The original game featured character art and elaborate animations for chain reactions — here the focus is on the pure arithmetic cascade.
