RPG-005 · POST APOC RPG

RPG — Role-Playing Prototype #005 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Lead 4 Desert Rangers through a post-apocalyptic wasteland. Fight mutants, use skills, investigate the mutant attacks.

  • Arrows — Move party
  • Space — Interact / advance dialogue
  • A — Attack | S — Skill | D — Defend
  • I — Use item | F — Flee | 1-4 — Select ranger
  • R — Restart | ? — Info panel

Skills

Combat: Hit chance/damage. Medic: Heal HP. Lockpick: Open locks. Demolitions: Clear rubble. Barter: Better prices. Perception: Spot hidden items.

Quest

Visit the Lab, Raider Camp, Crater, then the Mutant Hive to stop the threat.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: The Bard's Tale (1985) → Wasteland (1988) → Fallout (1997) → Wasteland 2 (2014)

Wasteland (1988), by Brian Fargo at Interplay, was among the first RPGs with a persistent, reactive world. Players controlled Desert Rangers navigating moral dilemmas in post-nuclear Arizona.

Its skill system let players bypass combat via lockpicking, demolitions, or persuasion. Wasteland's DNA lives on in Fallout and Wasteland 2 (2014).

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1980-1993)

Contemporary AI: Expert systems, probabilistic reasoning, early decision theory.

Hidden Intelligence: Wasteland's skill system is a probabilistic outcome engine: P(success) = f(skill_level, difficulty, roll), mirroring utility functions in decision-theoretic AI. Party skill allocation mirrors the explore-exploit tradeoff in multi-armed bandits.

The Convergence: Modern game AI uses Monte Carlo simulation and RL for difficulty curves — automating what Wasteland's designers calibrated by hand.

If AI Had Designed This: An RL game master would adjust encounters based on party composition. Skill allocation guided by a recommendation system.

Core Mechanic

Post-apocalyptic party RPG with skill checks and tactical combat. Top-down exploration, 4 rangers, turn-based combat, probabilistic skills, resource management.
