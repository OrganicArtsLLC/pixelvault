How to Play

Choose a mobile (vehicle) and battle an AI opponent in turn-based artillery combat. Account for wind, aim carefully, and use your special shot wisely!

  • ↑ / ↓ — Adjust aim angle
  • Space (hold) — Charge power, release to fire
  • S — Fire special shot (limited uses)
  • ← / → — Move mobile
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Mobiles

  • Armor: Balanced stats. Normal shot deals solid damage. Special: Dual Shot (fires two projectiles).
  • Boomer: Arcing shots that bounce once before exploding. Special: Triple Bounce (bounces twice with larger blast).
  • Lightning: Fast straight shot, less affected by wind. Special: Beam (piercing laser line).

Tips

  • Wind: Check the wind arrow at the top — it pushes projectiles during flight.
  • Terrain: Explosions destroy terrain. Drop enemies into pits!
  • Specials: You only get 2 special shots per game — use them at key moments.
  • Movement: Limited movement per turn — reposition to get a better angle.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Scorched Earth (1991) → Worms (1995) → GunBound (2003, Softnyx)

GunBound (건바운드) was developed by Korean studio Softnyx and launched in 2003, quickly becoming one of the most popular online games in Asia with over 110 million registered users. It combined the classic artillery formula with vehicle selection, creating a meta-game layer where players chose "mobiles" — each with unique shot types, stats, and special abilities.

Unlike Worms' character-based approach, GunBound's vehicle-RPG element meant players developed expertise with specific mobiles, creating diverse playstyles and team compositions in multiplayer. The game pioneered the free-to-play model with cosmetic microtransactions that many modern games would later adopt.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 5 — Deep Learning (2006–present)

Hidden Intelligence: GunBound's vehicle selection creates a meta-game that mirrors ensemble methods in machine learning. Each mobile represents a different "model architecture" — Armor is a balanced generalist, Boomer excels at indirect attacks, Lightning dominates in low-wind conditions. Optimal play requires matching your mobile to the situation, just as ML practitioners select models suited to their data.

The Convergence: The AI opponent must solve trajectory optimization under uncertainty (wind), with the added complexity of mobile-specific shot physics. This is a constrained optimization problem where the constraints change based on vehicle choice — analogous to how transfer learning must adapt pretrained models to new domains.
