PHY-003 · LUNAR LANDER

PHY — Physics Prototype #003 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Land the spacecraft safely on a flat landing pad. Control thrust and rotation against lunar gravity. Land gently (low velocity) to score points. Crash too hard and it's over.

  • ↑ or W — Main thruster (burns fuel)
  • ← → or A D — Rotate
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Fuel is finite: Every thrust burns fuel. Plan your descent carefully.
  • Landing speed: Must be under 2.0 m/s vertical and 1.0 m/s horizontal for safe landing.
  • Angle matters: Must be nearly upright to land safely.
  • Bonus pads: Smaller pads are worth more points (2x, 3x multiplier).

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Lunar Lander (1979, Atari) → Thrust (1986) → Kerbal Space Program (2011)

Lunar Lander (1979) by Atari was one of the first vector-graphics arcade games. Based on a 1969 text-mode simulation (Lunar by Jim Storer), it challenged players to manage thrust, fuel, and gravity for a soft landing. The original used a thrust lever for analog control.

The game distills spaceflight to its essence: Newton's laws, fuel management, and precision. There are no enemies, no time pressure beyond fuel — just physics and nerve.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 2 — Expert Systems (1972–1980)

Contemporary AI: Optimal control theory was well-developed (Pontryagin, Bellman). NASA had already solved the powered descent guidance problem analytically for Apollo (1969).

Hidden Intelligence: Lunar Lander is a continuous control problem with a hard constraint (fuel) and soft constraints (landing velocity, angle). NASA's real landing guidance used optimal control mathematics — the game is a human-playable version of the same problem. The player becomes an analog computer, solving differential equations through feel.

The Convergence: OpenAI Gym's LunarLander-v2 is one of the most popular RL benchmarks. Every RL student trains an agent on essentially this game. The 1979 arcade game became a canonical AI training environment 40 years later.

If AI Had Designed This: Procedurally generated terrain with difficulty curves optimized by the same optimal control math NASA used — fuel-optimal trajectories as the scoring rubric.

Classification

Series: PHY — Physics

Games where Newtonian mechanics are the primary challenge. Lunar Lander strips physics to its purest form: gravity, thrust, inertia.

Core Mechanic

Thrust vs gravity with finite fuel. Continuous control. Rotation for thrust direction. Resource depletion creates urgency. Landing speed threshold determines success.
