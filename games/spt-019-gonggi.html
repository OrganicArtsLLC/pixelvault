How to Play

Gonggi is a traditional Korean stone-tossing game played with 5 small stones. Toss one stone into the air, pick up stones from the ground, and catch the tossed stone before it lands!

  • Click a ground stone to select it as the toss stone, then it flies up
  • While the stone is in the air, Click ground stones to pick them up
  • The tossed stone must be caught before it lands — if you miss, you fail the round
  • Round 1 (알기) — Pick up 1 stone at a time (4 tosses)
  • Round 2 (두기) — Pick up 2 stones at once (2 tosses)
  • Round 3 (세기) — Pick up 3 stones at once (1 toss + 1 toss for remaining 1)
  • Round 4 (네기) — Pick up all 4 remaining stones at once
  • Round 5 (날기) — Toss all 5 up, catch on back of hand
  • R — Restart game

History

Gonggi (공기놀이, also romanized as gongi or gongginori) is one of Korea's oldest and most beloved traditional games, played for centuries by children and adults alike. The game uses five small stones — originally pebbles gathered from riverbeds, later manufactured as colorful plastic pieces.

Gonggi belongs to the worldwide family of "jacks" or "knucklebones" games, with parallels in ancient Greek astragaloi (knucklebone tossing, ~3000 BCE), Japanese otedama, and the Western game of jacks. The Korean version emphasizes sequential rounds of increasing difficulty, creating a natural progression from simple to challenging.

The game remains deeply embedded in Korean culture — it appears in school playgrounds, variety shows, and even competitive tournaments. The satisfying tactile rhythm of toss-grab-catch has kept it alive across generations.

AI Archaeology

Era 2 — Heuristic

Gonggi's AI challenge is minimal from a strategic standpoint — the game is almost entirely about human dexterity and timing. A heuristic AI needs only to model the timing window (time between toss and landing) and execute pickups within that window. The real challenge is for human players: coordinating visual tracking of the airborne stone with precise hand movements to grab ground stones.

The progressive round structure is an elegant difficulty curve — each round demands picking up more stones in the same time window, naturally pushing players to develop faster coordination.
