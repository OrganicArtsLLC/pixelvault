SIM — Simulation Prototype #010 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Click the central crystal to generate energy. Spend energy on upgrades that produce energy automatically. Watch numbers grow exponentially.

  • Click the crystal to generate energy
  • Click upgrade buttons to purchase producers
  • R — Restart (full reset)
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Upgrades

  • Cursor — 0.1/s each. Cheap automated clicker.
  • Worker — 1/s each. Human-scale production.
  • Factory — 8/s each. Industrial output.
  • Lab — 47/s each. Research-driven generation.
  • Portal — 260/s each. Interdimensional harvesting.

Prestige

Once you reach 1 million lifetime energy, press R to reset with a permanent multiplier. Each prestige level adds +10% to all production.

History & Lineage

Progress Quest (2002) by Eric Fredricksen was the proto-idle game, satirizing RPG grinding by automating it entirely. Cookie Clicker (2013) by Julien "Orteil" Thiennot turned the joke earnest — clicking a cookie became a genuine compulsion loop that spawned an entire genre. Universal Paperclips (2017) by Frank Lantz, director of the NYU Game Center, wrapped the mechanic in an AI alignment parable. Antimatter Dimensions (2017) by Hevipelle pushed the math to its limit with nested infinity layers.

The idle genre proves that progression itself — not challenge, not narrative, not skill — is the atomic unit of game satisfaction.

AI Archaeology

Idle games are reward-shaping experiments stripped bare. The exponential growth curve with diminishing returns per upgrade tier models the multi-armed bandit problem: allocate limited resources across options with different payoff profiles. Cookie Clicker's prestige system is transfer learning — sacrifice current progress for a permanent multiplier, exactly like resetting a neural network with better initialization weights.

The genre's core loop (earn → spend → earn faster) mirrors the RL training cycle. The player IS the optimizer, manually tuning hyperparameters (which upgrade to buy) to maximize a reward signal (resources per second). AI Era 6 because the exponential scaling and reward shaping are pure deep learning patterns.

Prototype Notes

Stripped to essentials: one resource, five upgrade tiers, prestige. No save system (session only). The crystal visual and particle effects provide the satisfying click feedback that makes idle games work. Cost scaling at 1.15× per purchase follows the Cookie Clicker formula.
