How to Play

Drop water balloons to trap your opponents! Last player standing wins.

  • ← ↑ ↓ → — Move
  • Space — Drop water balloon
  • Balloons burst after 2 seconds in a cross pattern
  • Getting hit traps you in a water bubble — mash Space to escape!
  • Destroy soft blocks to find power-ups
  • Power-ups: +Range (longer blasts), +Balloon (carry more), +Speed (move faster), +Kick (push balloons)
  • Defeat all 3 AI opponents to win
  • R — Restart game

History

Crazy Arcade (크레이지 아케이드, also known as BnB — Boom and Bust) was released by Nexon in 2001 and became Korea's most beloved casual online game for over a decade. Built on Bomberman's core mechanics, it replaced bombs with water balloons and adopted a bright, cute aesthetic that appealed to Korea's PC bang (internet cafe) culture. At its peak, millions played simultaneously. The game proved that a well-localized, culturally-tuned adaptation of a classic mechanic could outperform the original in a specific market — a lesson the Korean games industry has applied repeatedly since.

AI Archaeology

AI Era 5 — Deep Learning

Balloon bombing requires planning multi-step traps — predicting opponent movement and cutting off escape routes. Each balloon placement reshapes the navigation graph, making this a dynamic constraint satisfaction problem. AI agents must balance aggression (trapping opponents) with self-preservation (not boxing themselves in). Korea's decade of competitive BnB play generated enormous datasets of human strategic decision-making in grid-based environments.

Prototype Notes

13x11 grid with destructible soft blocks and indestructible pillars in an alternating pattern. Water balloons burst in cross patterns after a 2-second fuse. Blast hits trap players in bubbles — mash Space to escape or be eliminated. Four power-up types hidden in soft blocks. Three AI opponents with pathfinding that avoids active balloon blast zones. The kick power-up adds a strategic dimension — push balloons down corridors to reach distant opponents.
