RPG-002 · DUNGEON DASH

RPG — Role-Playing Game Prototype #002 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Move into enemies to fight them — there is no attack button. Your approach angle determines how much damage you deal and take. Defeat three bosses to win.

  • ↑ ↓ ← → — Move (bump enemies to attack)
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Bump Combat

  • Off-center hit: Clip the enemy's edge — deal 2x damage, take 0.5x.
  • Head-on hit: Walk straight into center — both deal/take full damage.
  • From behind: Hit the enemy's back — deal 3x damage, take 0.
  • Standing still: HP regenerates slowly (the Ys signature mechanic).

Tips

  • Grind: Fight weak enemies to level up before entering harder zones.
  • Angle: Always try to clip enemies off-center. Head-on is wasteful.
  • Regen: Stand still to recover HP between fights.
  • Inn: Walk into the inn building in town to fully heal.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Ys: Ancient Ys Vanished (1987, Nihon Falcom) ← Hydlide (1984, T&E Soft) ← Ultima (1981)

Ys (1987) by Nihon Falcom removed the attack button entirely. In an era when action RPGs required complex input, Ys reduced combat to a single verb: move. The player's approach angle determines damage — off-center hits deal more damage and take less. This creates a spatial combat system where positioning IS the entire skill.

The HP regeneration mechanic (heal by standing still) creates a natural risk/reward rhythm: push forward aggressively, then pause to recover. This pacing mechanic predates modern "regenerating health" by two decades.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1980-1987)

Contemporary AI: Expert systems, early neural network revival. Real-time stat resolution in games was novel.

Hidden Intelligence: Ys encodes all tactical decisions into a continuous movement space. The approach angle creates a damage function that maps spatial position to combat outcome — this is a continuous state-action mapping, the same structure that modern policy gradient methods optimize. The player learns this mapping through embodied experience, not instruction.

The Convergence: Bump combat is essentially a reward-shaping problem: the game rewards spatial precision (off-center hits) over brute force (head-on). Modern RL agents trained on Ys-like games would discover the same angled approach strategy that human players learn intuitively.

If AI Had Designed This: Procedurally tuned enemy stat curves per zone, optimized level-up thresholds to maintain "flow" (not too easy, not too hard), and dynamic HP regen rates based on player skill estimation.

Classification

Series: RPG — Role-Playing Game

Stat-driven progression with real-time spatial combat. Ys strips the RPG to its essence: move, grow, overcome.

Core Mechanic

Bump combat with angle-dependent damage. No attack button. Off-center hits deal bonus damage. HP regenerates when still. Level progression with stat growth. Overworld with zone-based difficulty.
