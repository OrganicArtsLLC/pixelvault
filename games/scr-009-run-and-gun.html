SCR-009 · RUN AND GUN

SCR — Scrolling Prototype #009 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Run, jump, and gun your way through a side-scrolling gauntlet of soldiers, snipers, and turrets. Reach the boss at the end of the level and destroy its weak point to win. One hit kills you.

  • ← → — Move left/right
  • ↑ ↓ — Aim up / crouch (duck)
  • J — Jump
  • Space — Shoot (8-directional aiming)
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • One-hit death: Any enemy bullet or contact kills you instantly. Stay mobile.
  • 8-way aiming: Combine arrow keys while shooting to aim diagonally, straight up, or at angles while jumping.
  • Power-ups: Shoot the flying pods to release weapon letters. Walk into them to equip: S=Spread, M=Machine Gun, L=Laser.
  • Jump over bullets: Many enemy shots can be jumped over. Use vertical space.
  • Boss weak point: The red core. Hit it while dodging the boss's attacks.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Contra (1987, Konami) → Super C (1988) → Contra III (1992) → Metal Slug (1996, SNK)

Contra (1987) defined the run-and-gun genre with its relentless pace, one-hit deaths, and cooperative two-player action. The game demanded precision platforming combined with constant shooting — a design that made every second feel life-or-death.

The power-up system (Spread, Machine Gun, Laser) gave players meaningful tactical choices. The Spread shot became iconic — its wide coverage made it the most desirable weapon, creating a risk/reward dynamic around collecting power-ups mid-combat.

The Konami Code (Up Up Down Down Left Right Left Right B A) originated with Gradius but became famous through Contra, granting 30 lives to offset the punishing difficulty.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1980-1993)

Contemporary AI: Expert systems, early neural networks. Pattern recognition was becoming formalized.

Hidden Intelligence: Contra's enemy spawning uses carefully choreographed patterns — soldiers run in from specific screen edges at scripted intervals, turrets fire with predictable timing, and bosses cycle through phased attack sequences. This is authored choreography, not AI, but it creates the illusion of intelligent opposition through precise timing design.

The Convergence: The wave-based spawning system is essentially a finite state machine with timed transitions. Modern game AI uses similar state machines but with learned transition probabilities. Contra's designers hand-tuned what reinforcement learning now automates.

If AI Had Designed This: Adaptive difficulty that reads the player's skill level from death patterns and adjusts spawn rates and bullet density. The Spread shot would be placed strategically before difficulty spikes — something speedrunners already exploit in the hand-authored version.

Core Mechanic

Side-scrolling run-and-gun with 8-directional shooting. One-hit death. Power-up weapons. Jump-and-shoot simultaneously. Choreographed enemy waves. Boss with phased attacks.

What This Prototype Tests

  • One-hit death — does instant death feel fair with enough player agency?
