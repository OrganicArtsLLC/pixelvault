How to Play

Planning Phase

Build your army by buying units from the shop and positioning them on your battle grid.

  • Click a shop unit — Buy it (costs gold, goes to bench)
  • Drag a unit from bench to grid — Place on board
  • Drag a unit on the grid — Reposition
  • Right-click a unit — Sell for full gold refund
  • Space — Start battle when ready
  • R — Restart the game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Battle Phase

Units fight automatically. Melee units walk toward enemies; ranged units shoot from distance. Watch your strategy play out!

Synergies

  • 2+ Warriors — All units gain +20% HP
  • 2+ Archers — Ranged units gain +25% damage
  • 2+ Mages — Spell damage splashes to adjacent enemies
  • 2+ Tanks — Tanks gain +30% HP and taunt nearby enemies
  • 2+ Healers — Healing amount doubled
  • 2+ Assassins — Assassins gain +50% crit damage

Economy

  • Earn base gold each round plus 10% interest on savings (max 5 bonus)
  • Win streaks and lose streaks grant bonus gold
  • Buy 3 copies of the same unit to merge into a 2-star version (doubled stats)

History & Lineage

In January 2019, Drodo Studio released Auto Chess as a Dota 2 custom game mode, and within weeks it had millions of players. The core innovation: remove direct combat control entirely, forcing players to think at the level of system design — composition, positioning, economy. The genre went from mod to mainstream in months, spawning Teamfight Tactics (2019, Riot Games), Dota Underlords (2019, Valve), and Super Auto Pets (2021, Team Wood Games).

The genre draws from gacha mechanics, deck-building, and chess-like positional strategy, creating a uniquely modern hybrid.

AI Archaeology

Auto-battlers are fascinating from an AI perspective because the player IS the AI designer. You don't control units — you design a system and watch it execute. This mirrors the fundamental challenge of AI alignment: specifying what you want rather than how to do it. The emergent combat behaviors (focus-fire, kiting, ability timing) arise from simple rules, much like complex AI behavior emerges from simple reward specifications.

The genre also embodies multi-agent systems: each unit is an autonomous agent with local perception and simple decision rules, yet the collective behavior can be surprisingly sophisticated.

Prototype Notes

This prototype focuses on the core loop: shop → position → fight → repeat. The AI opponent generates increasingly difficult armies. Six unit types with distinct combat behaviors create meaningful composition choices. The 5×3 grid keeps positioning decisions manageable while still strategic.

Synergy bonuses reward specialization but the shop's randomness forces adaptation. The economy system (interest, streaks) adds a layer of long-term planning over the tactical decisions.
