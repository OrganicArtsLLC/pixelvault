How to Play

Flick your striker disc to pocket carrom men of your assigned color (white or black). Pocket all 9 of your pieces plus the queen to win!

  • Click + Drag — Aim the striker and set power (longer drag = more power)
  • Release mouse to flick the striker
  • Pocket the red Queen, then immediately "cover" it by pocketing one of your own pieces on the next shot
  • If you pocket your piece, you get another turn
  • If the striker goes in a pocket, your turn ends and one pocketed piece returns to center
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

History

Carrom (also spelled carom or karrom) is a tabletop game of South Asian origin, believed to date back to 18th-century India. It spread throughout South and Southeast Asia, becoming hugely popular in India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Pakistan, and Nepal. The game is often called "finger billiards" — players flick a heavier striker disc to knock lighter pieces into corner pockets.

The International Carrom Federation, founded in 1988 in Chennai, India, standardizes rules and organizes world championships. Carrom boards are a fixture of homes, clubs, and street corners across South Asia, representing one of the most-played tabletop games in the world.

AI Archaeology

Era 3 — Expert Systems

Carrom's AI challenge sits at the intersection of physics simulation and combinatorial planning. An expert system can calculate optimal angles for direct pocketing shots using reflection geometry, but multi-disc collisions introduce chaos that makes long-term planning difficult. The continuous state space (angle, power) combined with discrete outcomes (pocketed or not) creates a hybrid optimization problem.

Modern approaches would use Monte Carlo simulation to evaluate shot candidates, but even simple heuristic AI — aim at the nearest piece aligned with a pocket — provides a reasonable opponent.
