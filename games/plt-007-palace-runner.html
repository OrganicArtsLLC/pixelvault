PLT-007 · PALACE RUNNER

PLT — Platformer Prototype #007 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Navigate the dungeon to escape before time runs out. Avoid traps, defeat guards with your sword, and find the exit on each level.

  • ← → — Run left/right
  • ↑ ↓ — Climb up/down ledges
  • Space — Jump (running jump or standing jump)
  • Z or Shift — Careful step / grab ledge / parry (in combat)
  • X — Draw sword / attack (in combat)
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Careful step (Z): Walk slowly to test for loose floor tiles. Falling floors crumble when you run over them.
  • Ledge grab: When falling past a ledge, hold Z to grab on. Press Up to pull yourself up.
  • Spikes: Spike pits are instant death. Look before you leap.
  • Guillotines: Time your passage through timed guillotine traps.
  • Sword combat: When near a guard, press X to draw your sword. Use Left/Right to advance/retreat, X to strike, Z to parry.
  • Potions: Collect potions on the ground to restore health.
  • Timer: You have 60 minutes (simplified). Move quickly but carefully.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Karateka (1984, Jordan Mechner) → Prince of Persia (1989, Broderbund) → Out of This World (1991) → Oddworld (1997) → Prince of Persia: Sands of Time (2003)

Jordan Mechner spent four years developing Prince of Persia (1985-1989). He filmed his brother David running and jumping, then rotoscoped the footage frame by frame. The result was unprecedented fluid movement on the Apple II — a character that felt human.

Mechner's earlier Karateka (1984) pioneered cinematic game techniques. Prince of Persia elevated every aspect — more complex animation, intricate level design, and the 60-minute timer that transformed exploration into speed-running. It launched the "cinematic platformer" subgenre where the Prince moved as a physical body with momentum, stumbling, and ledge-grabbing.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (through late 1980s influence)

Contemporary AI: Neural networks were in their second winter. Expert systems were losing credibility. Connectionists and symbolists debated architecture.

Hidden Intelligence: Prince of Persia's rotoscoped animation encoded an implicit physical model of human movement — momentum, stumbling, ledge-grabbing, recovery. This prefigured the motion-capture and physics-based animation that AI systems now generate procedurally.

The Convergence: Modern AI locomotion controllers (deep RL) rediscovered what Mechner encoded by hand: realistic movement requires transition states, momentum, and environment-aware animation. Rotoscoping was manual "data-driven animation."

If AI Had Designed This: Procedurally generated dungeons with guaranteed solvability. Adaptive trap timing calibrated to skill. RL-driven character animation replacing hand-rotoscoped frames.

Classification

Series: PLT — Platformer

Prince of Persia is the foundational cinematic platformer. Gravity, momentum, ledge interaction, and physical consequence define every action. The room-based level structure creates discrete puzzle-action challenges.

Core Mechanic

Cinematic platformer with physics-based movement, trap navigation, and contextual sword combat. Running jump distance depends on momentum. Ledge-grabbing as a core traversal mechanic. Environmental hazards (spikes, guillotines, falling floors) as puzzle elements. Simple sword dueling against guards. Global timer creating urgency.
