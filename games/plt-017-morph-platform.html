PLT — Platformer Prototype #017 ★★★★☆

How to Play

You are a squishy blob in a deformable world. Dash into soft platforms to reshape them and reach collectible orbs. The world is your clay.

  • ← → — Move left/right
  • ↑ ↓ — Aim dash direction
  • Space — Jump
  • Z — Dash (in aimed direction)
  • R — Reset platform deformations
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Deformation Rules

  • Green platforms: Soft — deformable by dashing into them
  • Grey platforms: Hard — cannot be deformed
  • Dash up from below a soft platform to push its surface up (create a bump)
  • Dash down from above to create an indentation
  • Dash sideways to push the platform edge

Tips

  • Create ramps to reach high places
  • Make indentations to shelter from hazards or as stepping stones
  • Press R to reset all deformations if you get stuck
  • Collect all orbs to advance to the next level

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Semblance (2018, Nyamakop) — a pioneering indie platformer from Cape Town, South Africa

Semblance was created by Nyamakop, a two-person studio founded by Ben Myers and Cukia “Sugar” Kimani in Cape Town, South Africa. Released in 2018 for Nintendo Switch and PC, it became one of the first African-developed games on a major Nintendo platform.

The game’s central innovation is deformable terrain: instead of the player adapting to the world, the world adapts to the player. Soft, clay-like platforms can be pushed, pulled, and reshaped by the player’s dash ability, turning every puzzle into a creative exercise in world-sculpting.

Semblance drew praise for its elegant simplicity and its roots in South African indie game culture. Nyamakop emerged from the small but vibrant Cape Town game development scene, and the game’s success helped put African indie games on the global map.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 6 — Generative AI (2020–present)

Contemporary AI: Large language models, diffusion models, generative design systems.

Insight: Semblance inverts the platformer contract — instead of navigating fixed geometry, the player reshapes it. This mirrors generative AI’s fundamental shift from consuming content to producing it. The deformable world is essentially a player-authored generative design space, where solutions emerge from creative manipulation rather than predetermined paths. Each player’s solution is unique, much like how generative models produce varied outputs from the same prompt.

Classification

  • Primary: Platformer (deformable puzzle)
  • Secondary: Puzzle, physics-based
  • Input: 4-directional + jump + dash
  • Perspective: Side-on, single screen per level

Prototype Notes

This prototype implements the core deformable-platform mechanic across 6 puzzle levels. Soft (green) platforms respond to dash impacts by deforming their surface geometry, while hard (grey) platforms remain fixed. Each level requires creative platform manipulation to collect all orbs and advance.
