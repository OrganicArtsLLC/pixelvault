SIM-004 · EMPIRE BUILDER

SIM — Simulation Prototype #004 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Build an empire from a single settler. Found cities, research technologies, train military units, and conquer or out-tech your AI rival.

  • Arrow keys or right-click drag — Scroll the map
  • Click — Select unit or city; click tile to move/attack
  • B — Build city (settler selected on valid tile)
  • T — End turn
  • 1-4 — Build unit in selected city (1=Settler, 2=Warrior, 3=Archer, 4=Horseman)
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Settle early: Found your first city quickly on grassland or plains for best production.
  • Explore: Send warriors to reveal the fog of war and find good city sites.
  • Tech up: Technologies unlock better units and increase city output. Research is automatic each turn.
  • Gold: Cities generate gold. Use it to rush-buy units (click the gold cost in city view).
  • Victory: Conquer all enemy cities OR research all 6 technologies first.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Empire (1977, Walter Bright) → Civilization (1991, Sid Meier) → Civilization II (1996) → Alpha Centauri (1999)

Sid Meier's Civilization (1991) synthesized board game mechanics (Risk, Diplomacy), Walter Bright's Empire, and SimCity's city management into the definitive 4X strategy game. The "one more turn" compulsion loop — where each turn reveals new possibilities — became one of gaming's most addictive feedback mechanisms.

The original ran on a 80x50 map with 14 civilizations and a tech tree of 72 advances. This prototype distills the core loop: settle, build, research, conquer.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 4 — Statistical Learning (1990-2000)

Contemporary AI: Neural networks resurging, genetic algorithms, multi-objective optimization entering practical use.

Hidden Intelligence: Civilization's AI must simultaneously optimize across competing objectives: military strength, economic growth, technological progress, territorial expansion, and diplomatic relationships. This is a multi-objective optimization problem that operations research had been formalizing for decades, but Meier implemented it as a set of competing heuristic priorities — essentially a hand-tuned Pareto frontier.

The Convergence: The tech tree is a directed acyclic graph of prerequisites — identical in structure to the dependency graphs used in project scheduling (PERT/CPM). The AI's challenge of choosing an optimal path through this graph anticipates modern planning algorithms.

If AI Had Designed This: Procedurally balanced maps using constraint satisfaction, AI opponents trained via self-play (as AlphaStar later achieved for StarCraft), and dynamically adjusted difficulty using player modeling.

Classification

Series: SIM — Simulation

Civilization is the quintessential 4X strategy simulation — managing an entire civilization's growth across millennia through exploration, expansion, exploitation, and extermination.

Core Mechanic

4X turn-based strategy with tech tree progression. Procedural map. City founding and management. Unit production and movement. Technology research. AI rival civilization. Fog of war.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Settle decision — does choosing where to build a city feel consequential?
  • Tech progression — does unlocking new capabilities create a sense of growth?
  • Expansion pressure — does the AI rival create urgency to expand?
  • One more turn — does the turn structure create compulsive engagement?

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. Simplified 4X with 30x20 tile map, 4 unit types, 6 technologies, fog of war, and one AI opponent. Captures the core explore-expand-exploit-exterminate loop in under 50KB.
