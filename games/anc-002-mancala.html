ANC-002 · MANCALA

ANC — Ancient Games Prototype #002 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Pick up all seeds from one of your pits and sow them one-by-one counter-clockwise. Collect the most seeds in your store (the large pit on the right).

  • Click a pit on your side (bottom row) or press 1-6
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Rules

  • Sowing: Pick up all seeds from a pit, drop one in each subsequent pit counter-clockwise.
  • Extra turn: If your last seed lands in your store, you go again.
  • Capture: If your last seed lands in an empty pit on your side, capture that seed and all seeds in the opposite pit.
  • Skip opponent's store: Never drop seeds in the opponent's store.
  • Game end: When one side is empty, the other player collects all remaining seeds.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Mancala/Oware (~700 CE) → Kalah (1940) → hundreds of regional variants

Mancala is a family of count-and-capture games played across Africa, Asia, and the Caribbean for over a millennium. The word comes from the Arabic "naqala" (to move). Over 800 variants exist worldwide.

The single verb — sow — creates emergent strategic depth. Pick up seeds, distribute. That's it. Yet from this one action come extra turns, captures, positioning, and multi-move planning chains. It is perhaps the purest demonstration that simple rules create infinite depth.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 1 — Symbolic AI (pre-1965)

Contemporary AI: The sowing mechanic predates computing. But the game structure — deterministic, perfect information, finite — is exactly what minimax with alpha-beta pruning was designed to solve.

Hidden Intelligence: Each sow is a discrete state transformation — pick up N seeds, redistribute across N positions. The resulting state depends on the current board configuration in non-trivial ways. Multi-step sowing chains (extra turns) create lookahead trees where planning 3-4 moves ahead is critical. This is a natural environment for tree search algorithms.

The Convergence: Mancala variants have been solved by computer for small board sizes. The game's depth-to-rule-complexity ratio is extraordinary — it proves that the search space can be enormous even with a single action type.

If AI Had Designed This: Optimized pit count and starting seeds to maximize the ratio of strategic depth to rule complexity — the result might be surprisingly close to Kalah(6,4).

Classification

Series: ANC — Ancient Games

Games that survived 1000+ years. Mancala's global distribution proves its design is culturally universal.

Core Mechanic

Seed sowing around pit circuit. Single verb (sow). Extra turns on store landing. Capture on empty pit landing. No randomness — pure strategy.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Capture mechanic — does the opposite-pit capture create tension?
  • Endgame — does the collection phase resolve cleanly?
