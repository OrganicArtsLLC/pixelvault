How to Play

Keep the wooden top (팽이) spinning as long as possible by whipping it at the right moment!

  • Space or Click — Whip the top to add spin
  • Left / Right — Reposition your whip stance (needed when wind blows the top off-center)
  • Watch the RPM meter — whip when spin is medium-low for the best boost
  • Whipping when already fast wastes energy with minimal effect
  • If the top slows too much, it wobbles and falls over — game over!
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

History

Paengi-chigi (팽이치기) is a traditional Korean winter game dating back centuries. Children would carve wooden tops and spin them on frozen ground or ice, keeping them going by striking them with a whip or cord. The game was especially popular during the Lunar New Year and cold winter months when the frozen ground provided an ideal smooth surface.

Top spinning is found across nearly every culture — from the Latin American trompo to the Jewish dreidel to the South Asian lattu. Korea's paengi tradition is distinctive for its emphasis on endurance and competitive whipping technique. Skilled players could keep tops spinning for extraordinary lengths of time, and competitions between neighborhoods were common.

AI Archaeology

Era 2 — Heuristic Bots

The core challenge is a timing optimization problem: when to whip for maximum effect given the current spin speed and friction decay curve. A simple heuristic — "whip when RPM drops below threshold X" — performs well, but the optimal threshold shifts as wind intensity increases, requiring adaptive behavior. The spatial component (repositioning to match wind-displaced top) adds a second dimension to the heuristic.
