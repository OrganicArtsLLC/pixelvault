SCR-004 · JUNGLE SQUAD

SCR — Scrolling Shooter Prototype #004 ★★★★☆

How to Play

You are a lone commando pushing upward through enemy territory. Move and aim independently — survive, destroy, advance.

  • WASD — Move (8 directions)
  • Arrow keys — Aim and shoot in that direction
  • Space — Throw grenade (limited ammo)
  • R — Restart
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • Don't fall behind: If you drop below the bottom of the screen, you die.
  • Grenades explode: They have area damage — use them on bunkers and groups.
  • Grab pickups: Ammo crates give grenades, stars give spread shot, boots give speed.
  • Bunkers are tough: They take multiple hits. Grenades are more effective.
  • Boss at the end: A large tank appears after ~6000px of scrolling. It has an HP bar.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Commando (1985, Capcom) → Ikari Warriors (1986, SNK) → Guerrilla War (1987)

Ikari Warriors (1986) by SNK introduced rotational joystick controls to the top-down run-and-gun genre. Unlike Commando, where you aimed in the direction you moved, Ikari Warriors let players rotate their aim independently — a radical control innovation that demanded dual cognitive tracking.

The game's forced vertical scroll created constant forward pressure. You couldn't stop to plan; you had to move, aim, and decide simultaneously. This was one of the first arcade games to demand genuinely parallel input processing from the player.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1978–1983, extending into mid-80s arcade design)

Contemporary AI: Multi-layer perceptrons existed but backpropagation was just being rediscovered (1986). Expert systems dominated practical AI. Real-time multi-output control was barely theorized.

Hidden Intelligence: The independent move/aim axes create a dual-output control problem. The player's brain must maintain two separate spatial models — one for navigation (avoid obstacles, stay on screen) and one for targeting (track enemies, lead shots). This mirrors multi-headed neural networks where separate output layers share a common representation but produce independent action streams.

The Convergence: Modern game AI agents (like OpenAI Five for Dota 2) face exactly this problem: simultaneous movement and targeting decisions from a shared world model. The cognitive architecture Ikari Warriors demands from human players presaged the multi-head attention mechanisms that would emerge decades later.

If AI Had Designed This: Adaptive difficulty that monitors both movement efficiency and aim accuracy independently, tuning enemy density and placement to challenge whichever skill the player is weaker at — forcing balanced dual-stream improvement.

Classification

Series: SCR — Scrolling Shooter

Games where the environment moves continuously. Ikari Warriors adds independent aiming to the vertical scrolling formula.

Core Mechanic

Dual-axis control with forced scroll. Independent movement and aiming. Vertical auto-scroll with increasing enemy density. Grenades for area damage. Power-ups for tactical variation.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Dual-input cognitive load — can players manage move + aim simultaneously?
  • Forced scroll pressure — does upward movement create urgency?
  • Grenade resource management — do limited grenades create meaningful decisions?
  • Enemy variety — do soldiers, bunkers, and jeeps feel distinct?

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. Procedural terrain with rivers, walls, bunkers, trees. Three enemy types plus a boss encounter. Independent move/aim is the core differentiator from Commando-style games.
