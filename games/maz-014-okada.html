How to Play

Ride your okada (motorcycle taxi) through the bustling streets of Lagos! Dodge traffic and pick up passengers to earn points.

  • ← → — Switch lanes (3 lanes)
  • Dodge yellow danfo buses, pedestrians, potholes, and hawkers
  • Pick up passengers standing on the roadside for +100 points
  • Speed increases as you travel farther
  • 3 lives — hitting an obstacle costs one life
  • R — Restart after game over

History

Okada (commercial motorcycle taxis) are a vital part of Lagos transportation, weaving through the legendary traffic jams (go-slow) of Africa's largest city. Maliyo Games, founded in Lagos in 2012 by Hugo Obi, was one of the first African game studios to create games rooted in African culture and daily life. Their work helped establish the Nigerian game development scene, proving that games about local experiences resonate with players worldwide. The danfo — Lagos's iconic yellow minibuses — are as much a part of the city's identity as the okada riders who navigate around them.

AI Archaeology

AI Era 6 — Generative

Traffic runner games model flow dynamics and obstacle avoidance in constrained lanes. The Lagos traffic setting introduces culturally specific obstacle types and behavioral patterns — danfo buses that stop unpredictably, hawkers weaving between vehicles, pedestrians crossing without warning — that challenge AI systems trained primarily on Western traffic assumptions. Procedural generation of culturally authentic urban environments is an emerging frontier in generative AI.

Prototype Notes

Top-down vertical scrolling with 3-lane road. Market stalls and street signs decorate the roadsides. Obstacles include danfo buses (wide, slow), pedestrians (cross lanes), potholes (instant damage), and hawkers with head-carried trays. Nigerian flag colors (green-white-green) appear in UI elements. Score combines distance traveled and passengers collected.
