MAZ — Maze Prototype #008 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Lead your blue team to capture the red flag and bring it home. First team to 3 captures wins!

  • W A S D — Move your player
  • Cross the midline to enter enemy territory
  • Touch the red flag to grab it, then bring it back to your base
  • Tag red players on YOUR side (left half) to send them to jail
  • Free jailed teammates by touching the jail area on the enemy side
  • If tagged while carrying the flag, you drop it — it returns to base after 5 seconds
  • You can only score if your own flag is safe at your base
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

History

Capture the Flag has been played as an outdoor game for centuries, formalized in the 19th century as a military training exercise. The digital version emerged through multiplayer FPS games — Team Fortress (1996) and Quake CTF popularized the mode, making it a staple of competitive gaming. The core tension — offense vs defense, individual glory vs team need — remains compelling across all formats.

AI Archaeology

AI Era 5 — Deep Learning

CTF requires agents to adopt roles (attacker, defender, support) and switch dynamically based on game state. This multi-agent coordination problem — where individual optimal play differs from team-optimal play — is a foundational challenge in cooperative AI research. DeepMind's 2019 CTF work showed that emergent team strategies arise from multi-agent reinforcement learning.

Prototype Notes

Arena divided by midline with maze-like walls. AI teammates use role-based behavior — attackers seek the enemy flag through gaps in walls, defenders patrol near the home flag. Jail and freeing mechanics add a rescue dimension. Dropped flags auto-return after 5 seconds to prevent stalemates.
