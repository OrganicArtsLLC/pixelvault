SIM-019 · SHOP KEEPER

SIM — Simulation Prototype #019 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Run an item shop in an RPG town. Buy low, sell high, and crawl dungeons for rare loot. You owe 10,000 gold — payments due every 5 days!

  • Shop Phase: Customers enter and want to buy items.
  • Click an item on the shelf to select it
  • + / - — Adjust price of selected item
  • Enter — Confirm sale to current customer
  • D — Go to dungeon (costs one time period)
  • N — End day / next day
  • W — Visit wholesaler to buy stock
  • Dungeon Phase: Explore, fight, collect loot.
  • Arrow keys — Move your character
  • Space — Attack enemies
  • Esc — Flee dungeon (keep collected items)

History

Recettear: An Item Shop's Tale was created by the Japanese doujin circle EasyGameStation in 2007. It inverted the classic RPG formula — instead of being the hero buying items, you're the shopkeeper selling them. The game combined shop management with action-RPG dungeon crawling and became a cult hit after its 2010 English localization by Carpe Fulgur.

Its iconic catchphrase "Capitalism, ho!" became a beloved meme. The game demonstrated that niche Japanese indie games could find massive Western audiences, paving the way for similar shop-sim hybrids.

AI Archaeology

The shop pricing mechanic is a repeated negotiation game — each customer interaction is a mini auction where the player must infer willingness-to-pay from behavioral cues. Modern AI approaches this through multi-agent reinforcement learning, where pricing agents learn optimal strategies through millions of simulated transactions. The dual shop/dungeon loop mirrors the exploration-exploitation tradeoff fundamental to AI decision-making.

Prototype Notes

This sketch captures the two-phase loop: manage your shop shelves and pricing, then dungeon-crawl for new stock. The debt mechanic adds pressure — miss a payment and it's game over. Simplified to core haggling + loot systems.
