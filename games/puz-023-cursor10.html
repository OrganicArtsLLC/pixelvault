PUZ-023 · CURSOR*10

PUZ — Puzzle Prototype #023 ★★★★☆

How to Play

You have 10 cursors, each with a 15-second timer. Move and click to interact with buttons and staircases across 8 floors. When your timer runs out, the next cursor begins — but your previous cursor replays as a ghost!

  • Click buttons to activate doors, platforms, and switches
  • Click the staircase to advance to the next floor
  • Ghost cursors replay your past movements and clicks automatically
  • Some buttons require a ghost to hold them while you proceed
  • Later floors need multiple ghosts cooperating simultaneously
  • R — Restart the entire game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

History

Cursor*10 was created by Yoshio Ishii of Nekogames in 2008, a Japanese Flash game developer known for minimalist browser games. The concept of cooperating with recordings of your past self was revolutionary — you literally become your own teammate. The game went viral and spawned a sequel (Cursor*10 2nd Session) and influenced time-manipulation mechanics in games like Braid and The Misadventures of P.B. Winterbottom.

AI Archaeology

Temporal Self-Play

Each ghost cursor is a frozen replay of a past "policy" — the sequence of decisions you made. This mirrors multi-agent self-play in reinforcement learning, where an agent trains by cooperating with copies of its past self. The challenge is that you must anticipate what your future selves will need while also leveraging what your past selves provide.

Emergent Coordination

No explicit communication exists between cursors. Coordination emerges from understanding the puzzle structure and planning across temporal instances. This is analogous to implicit coordination in multi-agent systems where agents must infer teammates' intentions from shared environmental context.

Prototype Notes

8 floors with escalating cooperation requirements. Ghost cursors shown as translucent colored arrows. Timer-based urgency creates tension between exploration and efficiency. The core insight: your past mistakes become your future tools.
