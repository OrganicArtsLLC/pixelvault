SIM-001 · TRADE VOYAGE

SIM — Simulation Prototype #001 ★★★★☆

How to Play

You are a trader sailing between ports in the South China Sea. Buy goods cheap, sell them dear, and retire with 10,000 gold.

  • 1-5 — Select menu option (Buy, Sell, Sail, Repair, Status)
  • ← → — Select goods or ports
  • ↑ ↓ — Adjust quantity
  • Enter — Confirm selection
  • Space — Fire cannons (during combat)
  • F — Flee (during combat)
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Trading

  • Each port has different base prices for Silk, Tea, Spice, and Jade.
  • Prices fluctuate each visit. Watch for bargains and sell high.
  • Your ship has limited cargo capacity (start: 50 units).

Dangers

  • Pirates: Attack while sailing. Fight with Space or Flee with F (lose cargo).
  • Storms: Random cargo loss while sailing.
  • Ship damage: Repair at Hong Kong to avoid sinking.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Lemonade Stand (1979) → Taipan! (1982) → Dope Wars (1998) → countless trading sims

Taipan! was created by Art Canfil in 1982 for the Apple II. Inspired by James Clavell's novel "Tai-Pan," it cast players as traders in 19th-century East Asia. The game pioneered the buy-low-sell-high loop with spatial movement and random events that would define an entire genre.

Before Taipan!, MECC's Lemonade Stand (1979) introduced economic simulation to computing. But Taipan! added geography, risk, and narrative tension. The player wasn't just optimizing — they were adventuring.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 2 — Expert Systems (1965-1980)

Contemporary AI: The price system is a stochastic model — base prices with random perturbation. The pirate encounters scale with time, creating an adaptive difficulty curve. These are the building blocks of economic modeling and risk assessment.

Hidden Intelligence: The port price system is a simplified spatial economic model. Each port has comparative advantages (Shanghai for Silk, Manila for Tea). The player discovers these advantages through exploration and builds a mental model of the price graph. This is implicit reinforcement learning — the human brain running a multi-armed bandit algorithm.

The Convergence: Modern algorithmic trading uses the same fundamental insight: buy where cheap, sell where expensive, manage risk in transit. Taipan! is a trading algorithm tutorial disguised as a game.

If AI Had Designed This: It might have optimized the price variance to maximize the information gain per voyage — ensuring each trip teaches the player something new about the market.

Classification

Series: SIM — Simulation

Games that model real-world systems. Trading, economics, resource management.

Core Mechanic

Buy-sell arbitrage across spatial nodes under uncertainty. Four ports, four goods, stochastic prices, risk events. Pure economic decision-making.
