How to Play

You command the defenders (gold pieces + the King). Your goal: escort the King to any corner square (marked with runes). The AI controls the attackers (red pieces) who must capture the King.

  • Select: Click one of your pieces to see legal moves highlighted.
  • Move: Click a highlighted square to move there. All pieces slide like chess rooks — any distance horizontally or vertically, no jumping.
  • Capture: Sandwich an enemy piece between two of yours (horizontally or vertically) to remove it.
  • King Capture: The King is captured when surrounded on all 4 sides by attackers (or 3 sides + the throne).
  • Win: Move the King to any corner square to escape and win!

History

Hnefatafl ("King's Table") was the premier strategy game of the Viking world, played from at least 400 CE across Scandinavia, Iceland, Britain, and as far as Byzantium. The game predates chess in Northern Europe by centuries. When chess arrived via Arab traders around 1000 CE, Hnefatafl gradually fell out of favor, though it survived in Lapland (as Tablut) until the 18th century, when Carl Linnaeus documented its rules during his 1732 expedition.

The game's asymmetric design is its genius — one side has numbers, the other has the objective. This creates fundamentally different strategic challenges for each player, a design principle that wouldn't be rediscovered in modern game design until the 20th century.

AI Archaeology

Classification

Asymmetric siege game. Two-player, perfect information, zero-sum with asymmetric objectives. The defender optimizes for King escape (shortest path to corner); the attacker optimizes for encirclement (blocking escape routes while closing the net).

AI Insight

Hnefatafl's asymmetry makes it a fascinating AI challenge. Unlike chess where both sides optimize toward the same goal (checkmate), here the evaluation function must be fundamentally different for each side. The attacker needs a "net tightness" heuristic while the defender needs an "escape route" heuristic. This duality prefigures modern asymmetric game AI.

Prototype Notes

9x9 board (Copenhagen-style layout). Player controls defenders (King + 8 warriors). AI uses minimax with alpha-beta pruning and role-aware evaluation. Special squares: throne (center, only King may occupy after leaving), corners (King escape targets).
