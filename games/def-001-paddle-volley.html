DEF-001 · PADDLE VOLLEY

DEF — Deflection Prototype #001 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Two paddles, one ball. First to 7 wins. Hit the ball at different positions on the paddle to change its angle.

  • W / S — Move left paddle
  • ↑ / ↓ — Move right paddle
  • Space — Serve
  • R — Restart

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Tennis for Two (1958) → Magnavox Odyssey (1972) → Atari Pong (1972)

Pong didn't invent the video game, but it invented the video game industry. Tennis for Two (1958) was the first electronic game with real-time interaction. The Magnavox Odyssey (1972) brought it home. Pong (1972) made it a business.

The core mechanic — deflection — is deceptively deep. The angle changes based on where the ball hits the paddle, giving players control over trajectory. Every game that uses a bouncing object (Breakout, Arkanoid, Peggle) descends from this simple loop.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 2 — Expert Systems (1972–1977)

Contemporary AI: Rule-based expert systems (MYCIN), pattern-matching (ELIZA). AI could encode expert knowledge as if-then rules but could not learn or adapt.

Hidden Intelligence: The opponent is a Y-position tracker — literally an if-statement, indistinguishable from contemporary AI capability. But the ball's angle physics creates more emergent intelligence than the opponent's code. The environment is smarter than the agent.

The Convergence: Both Pong and 1972 AI prove the same thing: a system doing one thing well beats a system doing everything poorly. Specialization was the winning strategy in both fields simultaneously.

If AI Had Designed This: Adaptive difficulty — the opponent tracks your preferred angles and covers them, forcing you to evolve your strategy.

Classification

Series: DEF — Deflection

Games built around redirecting a moving object. The player doesn't attack directly — they shape trajectories. This series explores what happens when your only verb is "redirect."

Core Mechanic

Angle-based deflection. Ball velocity increases on each hit. Hit position determines reflection angle. The accelerating speed creates escalating tension.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Deflection angle feel (does hit position affect trajectory satisfyingly?)
  • Speed escalation (how fast before it becomes pure reaction?)
  • Two-player local play (shared keyboard, competitive tension)

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch — a mechanical skeleton. The goal is to isolate and feel the deflection loop from the builder's perspective.
