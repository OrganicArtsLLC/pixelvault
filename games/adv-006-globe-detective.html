How to Play

A treasure has been stolen! Travel the world gathering clues to identify the thief and track them down. Issue a warrant when you know who did it, then catch them.

Tips

  • Gather clues: Visit locations in each city to learn about the suspect and their next destination.
  • Issue warrant: Once you know the suspect's features, issue a warrant from the travel menu. You must have a warrant to make an arrest.
  • Follow the trail: Destination clues tell you where the suspect went next. Follow quickly!
  • Watch the clock: Each flight costs hours. Run out of time and the suspect escapes.

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Where in the World Is Carmen Sandiego? (1985, Broderbund) → Where in Time (1989) → Carmen Sandiego franchise

Where in the World Is Carmen Sandiego? (1985) was a landmark educational game that made geography fun through detective gameplay. Players traveled the world following clues, using a reference book (included with the game) to decode hints about destinations and suspects.

The game spawned a massive franchise including TV shows, sequels, and reboots. Its core loop — gather clues, narrow suspects, chase the thief — remains a template for educational detective games.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 3 — Knowledge Explosion (1980–1995)

Contemporary AI: Expert systems, knowledge databases, early natural language processing.

Hidden Intelligence: Carmen Sandiego is constraint satisfaction in game form. Each clue eliminates suspects from the possibility space. The player performs a search through a graph of cities, using clue constraints to prune branches. This is exactly how AI constraint solvers work — reduce the search space through logical elimination until only one solution remains.

The Convergence: The game's database of geographic facts anticipates modern knowledge graphs. The suspect-elimination mechanic is a manual version of Bayesian inference — each clue updates the probability distribution over suspects.

If AI Had Designed This: Adaptive clue difficulty based on player knowledge. Dynamic suspect generation. Clues calibrated to the minimum information needed to uniquely identify the suspect at each stage.

Classification

Series: ADV — Adventure

Carmen Sandiego blends adventure exploration with deductive reasoning. The player navigates a world graph, gathering information to solve a mystery.

Core Mechanic

Geography detective with constraint satisfaction. Travel between cities. Gather clues at locations. Eliminate suspects. Issue warrant. Catch the thief before time runs out.

What This Prototype Tests

  • Clue-gathering loop — does investigating locations feel rewarding?
  • Suspect elimination — does narrowing down the suspect feel like detective work?
  • Time pressure — does the hour limit create urgency without frustration?
  • World navigation — does traveling between cities feel like a meaningful choice?

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. Procedurally generated cases with 8 suspects and 14 world cities. Clues are generated from suspect features and destination geography. Each case creates a trail of 4-6 cities.
