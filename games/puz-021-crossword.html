How to Play

Fill the crossword grid by solving across and down clues. Every white cell needs a letter.

  • Click a cell to select it (click again to toggle across/down)
  • Type a letter to fill the selected cell and advance
  • ← → ↑ ↓ — Navigate between cells
  • Tab — Jump to next clue
  • Backspace — Delete letter and move back
  • R — New puzzle
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Tips

  • The current word is highlighted in blue
  • The current clue is highlighted in the clue list
  • Intersecting letters help you solve harder clues
  • All letters turn green when the puzzle is complete

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Arthur Wynne's Word-Cross (1913) → New York Times Crossword (1942) → Dr.Fill AI (2021)

Arthur Wynne, a journalist from Liverpool, published the first crossword puzzle in the New York World on December 21, 1913. It was diamond-shaped and called a "Word-Cross." The New York Times began publishing crosswords in 1942, and under editor Will Shortz (since 1993), the NYT crossword became the gold standard. Crosswords are the most widely solved puzzle format in history, appearing in virtually every newspaper worldwide.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 5-7 — Statistical Learning to Generative-Agentic

Crossword construction is a constraint satisfaction problem: each word must be valid AND intersecting letters must agree. Dr.Fill, created by Matt Ginsberg, used NLP embeddings to solve crosswords at human-expert level, placing first at the American Crossword Puzzle Tournament in 2021. Modern LLMs can both solve and construct crosswords, combining vocabulary knowledge with spatial constraint reasoning.
