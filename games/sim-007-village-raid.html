SIM — Simulation Prototype #007 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Build a defensive base, then deploy troops to attack the enemy! Two alternating phases create a deep strategy loop.

  • 1 — Select Cannon (shoots ground troops, 2x2)
  • 2 — Select Archer Tower (shoots air+ground, 2x2)
  • 3 — Select Wall segment (blocks paths, 1x1)
  • 4 — Select Town Hall (must protect!, 3x3)
  • Left Click — Place building (build) / Deploy troop (attack)
  • Right Click — Remove building (build phase)
  • Tab — Switch to Attack Phase (from build) / Next Round (after attack)
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

Build Phase

  • Place defensive buildings on the grid to protect your Town Hall.
  • You get: 1 Town Hall, 3 Cannons, 2 Archer Towers, 15 Walls.
  • Walls block troop movement. Cluster defenses around your Town Hall.

Attack Phase

  • Deploy troops on the edges of the enemy base to destroy it.
  • 1 Barbarian — melee fighter, balanced stats
  • 2 Archer — ranged attacker, fragile
  • 3 Giant — tanky, targets defenses first
  • 4 Goblin — fast, targets Town Hall and resources
  • Get 3 minutes. Stars: 50% destruction = 1★, Town Hall = 2★, 100% = 3★

Defense Replay

After your attack, watch AI troops assault your base. Good placement matters!

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Rampart (1990, Atari) → Tower Defense (2000s) → Clash of Clans (2012, Supercell)

Clash of Clans (2012) by Supercell became one of the highest-grossing mobile games ever by splitting strategy into two asymmetric halves: designing a defensive base (an offline optimization puzzle) and attacking enemy bases (real-time tactical deployment). Rampart (1990) pioneered the build-then-defend loop, while the tower defense genre of the 2000s refined the idea of placing static defenses against waves of attackers.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 6 — Transformer Era

Clash of Clans' dual-phase design mirrors the train/inference split in machine learning. Base layout is an optimization problem solved offline (like training a model), while attack execution requires real-time decision-making against that frozen structure (like inference). Modern AI approaches to the game use reinforcement learning for troop deployment and evolutionary algorithms for base layout optimization.

Prototype Notes

Simplified to a grid-based system with four building types and four troop types. Pathfinding uses BFS on the grid. AI base generation creates randomized but structurally sound layouts. Defense replay simulates AI attacking with similar troop composition.
