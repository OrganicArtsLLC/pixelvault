How to Play

Play chess against an AI opponent. You are White (bottom). Click a piece to select it, then click a highlighted square to move. Captures trigger brief battle animations.

  • Select: Click one of your pieces to see valid moves highlighted in green.
  • Move: Click a highlighted square to move your piece there.
  • Capture: Move onto an opponent's square — watch the battle animation.
  • Promotion: Pawns reaching the 8th rank auto-promote to Queen.
  • Castling: Click the King, then click the castling destination (2 squares toward the Rook).
  • R — Restart game
  • ? — Toggle this info panel

History & Lineage

Ancestry: Chaturanga (~600 CE, India) → Shatranj (Persia) → Modern Chess (~1475, Europe) → Battle Chess (1988, Interplay)

Chess evolved from Chaturanga, a four-player Indian war game, through Persian Shatranj into the modern European game by the 15th century. The queen and bishop gained their modern powers around 1475, transforming the game from slow positional grinding into the dynamic tactical contest we know today.

Battle Chess (1988): Interplay's genius was realizing that chess pieces had personalities. The knight doesn't just take the pawn — it decapitates it. The rook transforms into a stone golem and crushes its opponent. The game was identical chess; the spectacle was entirely new. It sold millions and spawned an entire subgenre of animated chess games.

Shannon Number: The game tree complexity of chess is approximately 10^120 — more than the number of atoms in the observable universe. Yet humans play it intuitively.

AI Archaeology

AI Era: 1 — Symbolic AI (the foundational problem)

The Origin Story: Chess is not merely a testbed for AI — it defined AI. Claude Shannon's 1950 paper "Programming a Computer for Playing Chess" laid out minimax search, evaluation functions, and the concept of game trees. Alan Turing wrote a chess program by hand in 1952, executing it as a "paper machine" because no computer could run it yet.

The Milestones:

  • 1950: Shannon's paper establishes the theoretical framework
  • 1952: Turing's paper machine plays (badly)
  • 1958: Alpha-beta pruning discovered (independently, multiple times)
  • 1997: Deep Blue defeats Garry Kasparov — the moment AI entered public consciousness
  • 2017: AlphaZero learns superhuman chess in 4 hours from self-play alone

Why It Matters: Every search algorithm, every heuristic evaluation technique, every pruning strategy was first developed for chess. Minimax, alpha-beta, iterative deepening, transposition tables, quiescence search — the entire vocabulary of game AI was forged here.

If AI Had Designed Chess: It might have added randomness to reduce the advantage of brute-force calculation, or hidden information to require probabilistic reasoning. The fact that chess is fully deterministic and fully observable is what made it the perfect AI benchmark — and ultimately, the problem AI solved most completely.

Classification

Series: ANC — Ancient Games

Games with roots stretching back centuries or millennia. Chess is perhaps the most important game ever invented — a 1400-year-old design that remains the world's most popular competitive board game.

Core Mechanic

Perfect information, turn-based, piece-differentiated movement, capture by replacement. Each piece type has unique movement rules, creating emergent tactical and strategic complexity from simple components.

Prototype Notes

This is a sketch rated 4/5. Full standard chess rules including castling, en passant, and pawn promotion (auto-queen). AI uses minimax with alpha-beta pruning at depth 3. Capture animations are brief but give pieces personality in the Battle Chess tradition. Move history displayed alongside the board.
