SPT-014 · PÉTANQUE

SPT — Sports Prototype #014 ★★★★☆

How to Play

Throw metal boules to land closest to the small yellow cochonnet (jack). Outscore your AI opponent to reach 13 points first.

  • Click+Drag — Aim direction and set power (longer drag = more power)
  • Release — Throw the boule
  • R — New round
  • N — New game
  • ? — Toggle this panel

Strategy

  • Pointing — Gentle throw to place your boule near the cochonnet
  • Shooting — Hard throw to knock away an opponent's boule
  • The cochonnet can be moved if hit by a boule!
  • Each player has 3 boules per round. After all are thrown, the team with the closest boule scores 1 point per boule closer than the opponent's closest.

History

Pétanque was born in 1907 in La Ciotat, a coastal town near Marseille. Jules Lenoir, a former jeu provençal champion crippled by rheumatism, could no longer take the running throw. His friend Ernest Pitiot drew a circle on the ground and told him to throw with his feet planted — pieds tanqués in Provençal dialect. The simplified rules made the game accessible to all ages and spread it worldwide.

Today pétanque is played in over 160 countries. The Fédération Internationale de Pétanque et Jeu Provençal (FIPJP) governs the sport. Professional tournaments like the Mondial La Marseillaise draw 13,000+ teams. The game is a candidate for Olympic inclusion.

AI Archaeology

Era 3 — Expert Systems: Pétanque strategy mirrors classical decision theory. The point-vs-shoot choice is a minimax problem: pointing is low-risk/low-reward (incremental advantage), while shooting is high-risk/high-reward (removing opponent's advantage but potentially worsening position). Optimal play depends on score differential, remaining boules, and current board state — a dynamic programming problem analogous to poker pot odds.

Classification

  • Win condition: First to 13 points
