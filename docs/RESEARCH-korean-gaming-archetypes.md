# Research: South Korean Gaming — 25 Archetypes from the PC Bang to Mobile

**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Purpose:** 25 games representing South Korea's gaming ecosystem — from ancient
Korean folk games through the PC bang revolution to the mobile boom and indie scene.
Korea has one of the world's most sophisticated gaming cultures, yet most of its
innovations remain invisible to the West.

---

## The Korean Gaming Ecosystem

Korea's gaming culture is unique because:
- **PC bangs** (internet cafes) created a communal gaming culture unlike Western home gaming
- **Free-to-play** was pioneered in Korea (Nexon's *QuizQuiz*, 1999)
- **Esports** became a professional sport in Korea before anywhere else
- **Traditional games** survived urbanization through digital preservation
- **Rhythm games** evolved differently from Japan — Korea created its own lineage
- **Mobile gaming** exploded in Korea before the global smartphone era

## The 25 Selected Games

### Traditional Korean Games (6)

**1. spt-018-ddakji** — Ddakji (딱지) — Paper Card Slamming
- **Origin:** Traditional Korean children's street game
- **Mechanic:** Slam your folded paper card onto your opponent's card to flip it over.
Power and angle determine success. Famous from *Squid Game*.
- **Canvas:** Click-drag to aim + power, release to slam. Physics of card flipping.

**2. spt-019-gonggi** — Gonggi (공기) — Korean Jacks
- **Origin:** Korean 5-stone game (similar to jacks, played for centuries)
- **Mechanic:** Toss stones in the air, pick up ground stones, catch the tossed stone.
Progressive rounds increase difficulty (pick up 1, then 2, then 3, then 4, then 5).
- **Canvas:** Click to toss, click to pick, timing-based catch.

**3. spt-020-ssireum** — Ssireum (씨름) — Korean Wrestling
- **Origin:** Korean traditional wrestling (UNESCO Heritage, depicted in 4th century murals)
- **Mechanic:** Two wrestlers grip each other's satba (waist band). Push, pull, trip,
lift — first to make any body part above the knee touch the ground wins.
- **Canvas:** Button-mashing + timing. Power meter + direction = technique.

**4. spt-021-tuho** — Tuho (투호) — Arrow Throwing
- **Origin:** Korean aristocratic game (also Chinese/Japanese origin, but Korean version is distinct)
- **Mechanic:** Throw arrows (thin sticks) into a narrow-necked pot from a distance.
Precision aiming with arc physics. Scoring by which arrows land in the pot.
- **Canvas:** Aim angle + power to arc arrows into the pot opening.

**5. spt-022-jegichagi** — Jegichagi (제기차기) — Korean Hacky Sack
- **Origin:** Traditional Korean foot game (kicked shuttlecock)
- **Mechanic:** Keep a jegi (weighted shuttlecock) in the air using only your feet.
Kick timing + angle. Consecutive kicks build combo.
- **Canvas:** Click/Space to kick at the right moment. Gravity + timing.

**6. spt-023-paengi** — Paengi (팽이) — Top Spinning
- **Origin:** Traditional Korean winter game (spinning top with whip)
- **Mechanic:** Spin a top and keep it spinning by whipping it at the right moment.
The top slows from friction. Time your whip strikes to maintain spin.
- **Canvas:** Click to whip the top when it slows. Spin speed meter.

### PC Bang Era (7)

**7. fix-008-fortress** — Fortress (포트리스, 1999)
- **Creator:** CCR Inc. / Joyon (Korea)
- **Mechanic:** Team artillery — Korean Worms/Gorillas with destructible terrain, wind,
and special weapons. Up to 8 players in teams. Korea's most beloved casual PC game.
- **Canvas:** Set angle + power, fire projectile with wind. Terrain destruction.

**8. maz-017-crazyarcade** — Crazy Arcade / BnB (크레이지 아케이드, 2001)
- **Creator:** Nexon (Korea)
- **Mechanic:** Water-balloon Bomberman. Drop water balloons that burst in cross patterns.
Trap opponents, collect power-ups. Korea's #1 casual game for a decade.
- **Canvas:** Grid-based, drop balloons, avoid blasts, collect items.

**9. spt-024-pangya** — Pangya (팡야, 2004)
- **Creator:** Ntreev Soft (Korea)
- **Mechanic:** Anime-style golf with power bar + spin control. "Pangya!" shot for
perfect timing. Courses have fantasy elements (floating islands, underwater).
- **Canvas:** Power bar click, spin control, ball trajectory. 3 holes.

**10. spt-025-audition** — Audition Online (오디션, 2004)
- **Creator:** T3 Entertainment (Korea)
- **Mechanic:** Dance rhythm game — arrow key combos scroll; hit them in sequence
to the beat. More complex combos = more impressive dance moves.
Social dancing competition. Korea's most popular casual online game.
- **Canvas:** Arrow combo sequences, timing bar, dance animation.

**11. puz-039-quizquiz** — QuizQuiz (퀴즈퀴즈, 1999)
- **Creator:** Nexon (Korea) — the FIRST free-to-play online game
- **Mechanic:** Answer quiz questions correctly to earn puzzle attacks. Puzzle blocks
fall on opponent if you answer faster. Quiz + Tetris hybrid.
- **Canvas:** Quiz question, 4 choices, puzzle blocks fall on opponent on correct answer.

**12. sht-009-gunbound** — GunBound (건바운드, 2003)
- **Creator:** Softnyx (Korea)
- **Mechanic:** Mobile artillery — choose a "mobile" (vehicle), aim angle + power,
account for wind. Each mobile has unique special shots. Worms-like but with
RPG-style vehicle progression. 110 million registered users.
- **Canvas:** Angle + power + wind. Vehicle-specific special shots.

**13. spt-026-freestyle** — Freestyle Street Basketball (프리스타일, 2005)
- **Creator:** JCE (Korea)
- **Mechanic:** 3v3 street basketball. Timed button presses for dribble moves,
shots, blocks. Position-based play (guard, forward, center).
- **Canvas:** Side-view basketball with timing-based shooting.

### Rhythm Games (2)

**14. spt-027-pumpit** — Pump It Up (펌프 잇 업, 1999)
- **Creator:** Andamiro (Korea)
- **Mechanic:** 5-panel dance game — adds DIAGONAL arrows (upper-left, upper-right)
to DDR's 4 panels. Creates completely different step patterns.
Korea's answer to DDR — different enough to be its own genre.
- **Canvas:** 5 lanes (↖ ↙ ● ↘ ↗), notes scroll up, hit on beat.

**15. spt-028-djmax** — DJMAX (디제이맥스, 2004)
- **Creator:** Pentavision (Korea)
- **Mechanic:** Multi-lane rhythm — 4, 5, 6, or 8 keys. Notes fall from top.
Premium feel with music videos playing behind the notes. Korean rhythm
game masterpiece with original K-pop/electronic soundtrack.
- **Canvas:** Multiple falling note lanes, timing judgment.

### Mobile Era (3)

**16. plt-021-cookierun** — Cookie Run (쿠키런, 2013)
- **Creator:** Devsisters (Korea)
- **Mechanic:** Auto-running cookie — jump over obstacles, slide under barriers,
collect jellies. Cookie has special abilities. Massive in Korea and Asia.
- **Canvas:** Auto-scroll, Space to jump, Down to slide. Cookie character.

**17. scr-015-dragonflight** — Dragon Flight (드래곤플라이트, 2012)
- **Creator:** NEXT Games Korea
- **Mechanic:** Vertical scrolling shooter on a dragon mount. Drag finger/mouse to
move. Auto-fire. Collect gems to power up dragon's breath weapon.
Korea's first mobile gaming mega-hit (before Cookie Run).
- **Canvas:** Vertical shooter, mouse movement, auto-fire, gem collection.

**18. sim-021-windrunner** — Wind Runner (윈드러너, 2013)
- **Creator:** Actoz Soft (Korea)
- **Mechanic:** Endless runner with "wind" mechanics — gusts push you up.
Run, jump, glide on wind currents. Distance-based scoring with
animal companions that give bonuses.
- **Canvas:** Side-scrolling runner with wind/glide mechanic.

### Korean Indie (4)

**19. hyb-013-skul** — Skul: The Hero Slayer (스컬, 2021)
- **Creator:** SouthPAW Games (Korea)
- **Mechanic:** Roguelite where you swap SKULLS to change your entire moveset.
Each skull is a different class (warrior, mage, archer). Combine two
skull abilities. The swapping IS the strategy.
- **Canvas:** Side-scrolling combat, Space to swap skull = change abilities.

**20. plt-022-moastray** — MO:Astray (모아스트레이, 2019)
- **Creator:** Archpray (Korea/Taiwan)
- **Mechanic:** Play as a slime that launches to possess host bodies. Each host
has different abilities. Detach and re-possess to traverse levels.
Body-hopping as traversal.
- **Canvas:** Aim + launch slime, possess enemy, use their ability, detach, repeat.

**21. adv-016-replica** — Replica (레플리카, 2016)
- **Creator:** SOMI (Korea, solo developer)
- **Mechanic:** Found-phone narrative puzzle. You unlock a stranger's phone and
investigate their life. Read texts, check apps, make choices that affect
the surveillance state narrative. ALL gameplay on a simulated phone screen.
- **Canvas:** Simulated phone UI with apps, messages, gallery. Narrative choices.

**22. sim-022-kingdomwinds** — The Kingdom of the Winds (바람의나라, 1996)
- **Creator:** Nexon (Jake Song) — Korea's FIRST graphical online game
- **Mechanic:** Simplified: top-down RPG with real-time combat. Based on Korean
manhwa *Kingdom of the Winds* by Kim Jin. Walk, fight, quest.
- **Canvas:** Top-down exploration + combat. The ancestor of ALL Korean online games.

### Korean Classics (3)

**23. sim-023-astonishia** — Astonishia Story (어스토니시아, 1994)
- **Creator:** Sonnori (Korea) — Korea's first RPG developer
- **Mechanic:** Hex-grid SRPG with isometric view. Move units on hex tiles,
attack adjacent enemies. The Korean SRPG tradition began here.
- **Canvas:** Hex grid, move/attack units, turn-based tactical combat.

**24. adv-017-iljimae** — Adventures of Iljimae (일지매의 모험, 1987)
- **Creator:** Aproman (Korea) — Korean MSX era
- **Mechanic:** Side-scrolling action-platformer featuring the Korean folk hero
Iljimae (a Robin Hood figure who steals from corrupt nobles).
Korea's earliest original game character.
- **Canvas:** Platformer with sword combat, based on Korean folklore.

**25. plt-023-dungreed** — Dungreed (던그리드, 2018)
- **Creator:** Team Horay (Korea)
- **Mechanic:** Side-scrolling roguelite with dual-wielding and dash combat.
Procedural dungeons, shop between runs, multiple weapon types.
Fast, fluid action combat.
- **Canvas:** Side-scrolling combat, dash, attack, procedural rooms.
